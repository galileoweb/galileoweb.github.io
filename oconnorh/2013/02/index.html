Intermolecular Forces Lesson Part 2 (2/25/13):

February 22, 2013 Copernicus Uncategorized

Today we will be studying more about Intermolecular Forces (Section 14.1), and we will be moving on to how intermolecular forces affect the processes of evaporation, condensation (Section 14.4), and boiling point (Section 14.5). 

http://www.classzone.com/cz/books/woc_07/resources/applications/flipcards/woc_ch14.html

Write down the definitions for the following terms:  Intermolecular forces, Intramolecular forces, Dipole-dipole attraction, Hydrogen bonding, London dispersion forces, Vaporization (evaporation), Condensation, Vapor pressure

Go to http://www.ssag.sk/SSAG%20study/CHE/Intermolecular%20Forces.pdf

1.  a.  How much energy is required to break the attraction between HCl molecules in the liquid state?

    b.  How much energy is required to break the covalent bonds between H and Cl? 

    c.  Which forces are stronger, intermolecular or intramolecular?

2.  Why does the strength of intermolecular forces between atoms/molecules determine a subsance’s physical properties?

3.  List three attractive forces between neutral molecules.

4.  Under what conditions are dipole-dipole forces significant?

5.  Draw the diagram of “Attractive Dipole-Dipole Interactions” in your notebook.

6.  A molecule’s boiling point is (choose one)

            A.  directly proportional to its dipole moment (strength).

            B.  inversely proportional to its dipole moment (strength).

7.  True or False:  Electrons are distributed equally at all times around the nucleus.

8.  Under what conditions are London Dispersion Forces significant?

9.  Draw a diagram showing how one atom’s instantaneous dipole can induce a dipole on a neighboring atom and cause an attraction. 

10.  Why do larger molecules are greater polarizability?

11.  Which are stronger, hydrogen bonds or dipole-dipole forces?

Click on http://www.mikeblaber.org/oldwine/chm1045/notes/Forces/Vapor/Forces05.htm

12.  Describe in terms of vapor pressure what happens after water is poured into a closed container.

13.  In order to become vapor, what must the molecules overcome?

14.  Describe what is happening when equilibrium is reached. 

15.  What if molecules in the interior of the liquid decide to leave the liquid phase and go into the vapor phase?

16.    Water boils at 100°C as long as the atmospheric pressure is _________ torr (1 ______). 

17.  Water boils at less than 100°C at high altitudes because the atmospheric pressure is less than __________ torr (____ atm).
