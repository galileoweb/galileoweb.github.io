Pressure/Volume Lab

January 29, 2013 Copernicus Uncategorized

Boyle’s Law and Ideal Gas Law Experiment:

Open Microsoft Excel.

Enter your 10 data points for both volume and pressure.

Column A:  Pressure;  Column B:  Volume (of syringe).  Notice that this is opposite of your data table!!!!

  • Select/Highlight all the data you copied, including the column headings.
  • Click on the Insert menu;
  • Click on XY (Scatter) from the Chart type list (the picture where the dots are connected).  Choose the top right icon (the one with the dots connected with a smooth curve).  A small picture of your graph should come up.
  • Click on Layout menu at the top.  Choose a Chart Title (I don’t care which type).  Type in a Chart title:  “Pressure vs. Volume – [Type Your Name].”
  • Click on Axis Titles.  Choose Primary Horizontal Axis Title and then the “Title Below Axis” option.  Type:  “Pressure in kPa”  Go back up to the Axis Title under the Layout menu.  Choose Primary Vertial Axis Title and then Rotated Title.  Type:  “Volume of syringe in mL.”
  • Print out your graph.
  • Write in your notebook a brief description of the relationship between pressure and volume as demonstrated on your graph.  Then turn your graph in to me.

Now make and print out another graph, this time where Column A is 1/Pressure.  YOU NEED TO DIVIDE ALL YOUR NUMBERS:  ONE DIVIDED BY EACH PRESSURE.  OR, YOU CAN DO AN EXCEL FORMULA:  Type into each box of column A:  =1/Pressure Number.  Column B is still Volume of the syringe.  On this graph, before you print it out –> Click on Layout menu at the top, select Trendline, Click on the first box (Linear).  Then Click on Add Trendlines again and this time select the bottom option of the menu:  “More Trendline Options”.  Click the two bottom boxes:   Display Equation…, Display R-squared value….  Put a Title on it with your name!!! and Label your axes like I described above (but this time the horizontal axis should be labeled 1/Pressure.) Print out with your names on it!!!

Write in your notebook the volume of the tubing.  This is the y intercept.  If you did your second graph correctly, you should have an equation on it:  y=mx + b.  The b value is the volume of your tubing.

 

Chapter 13 Quiz Topics List

January 28, 2013 Copernicus Uncategorized

Chapter 13 Topics List:

-Know the meaning and temperatures (both K & C) of absolute zero.

-Know the molar volume of a gas at STP.

-Know the universal gas constant:  R = 0.08206 L-atm/K-mol

-Know the temperature and pressure at STP.

-Know the relationship between kinetic energy and temperature.

-Know the postulates of the Kinetic Molecular Theory of Gases (p. 426)

-Know the characteristics of an ideal gas

-Know how to use the two formulas that we used on the handout of 14 problems. 

-Know how to do a simple partial pressure problem  & how to use the molar volume of a gas to calculate moles

-Remember to convert units whenever necessary.

-You will be given the same Chemistry Reference Sheet that you will use on the California Standards Test.  On this sheet, you will have both formulas and all pressure unit conversions.

Ch.11 & 12 Test Study Guide

January 22, 2013 Copernicus Uncategorized

Know the definition of the following:  Wavelength, Photon, Quantized, Frequency.

Know the orbital shapes.

Know the maximum electrons per orbital.

Know the difference between orbital and principal energy levels.

Know how to do electron configurations.

Know how to determine which ions have the same electron configurations as certain atoms.

Know how many valence electrons are in Group 1A, 2A, 3A, 4A, 5A, 6A, 7A, 8A, and know names of major chemical families/groups.

Know relationship between valence electrons and chemical properties.

Know the period trends:  atomic radius, ionic size, ionization energy, and electronegativity.

Know the difference between ionic, covalent, and polar covalent bonds.

Know how to determine polarity in a compound (ans: do they have different electronegativities?)

Know the definition of polarity.

Know how to determine the number of lone pairs in a molecule.

Know how to determine the Lewis Structure of a molecule.

Know how to determine the molecular structure of a molecule (you will have a copy on the test)
