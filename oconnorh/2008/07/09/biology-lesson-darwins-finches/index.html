Biology Lesson – Darwin’s Finches

July 9, 2008 Copernicus Uncategorized

Evolution on the Internet: Darwin’s Finches

Go to http://www.pbs.org/wgbh/evolution/library/01/6/l_016_01.html

  1. Darwin thought _________________ __________________ operated over vast periods of time and couldn’t be observed, but Peter and Rosemary Grant observed natural selection during their research in the Galapagos Islands.
  2. Where did the Grants do their research (what island)?
  3. The small birds, or f __ __ __ __ __ __, that the Grants studied struggle for ___________, or actually different types of ___________. 
  4. What factor has influenced the availability of food on Daphne Major?
  5. The size and shape of the finches’ beaks are ______ that vary in adapting to environmental ______ or changes in those niches.
  6. Click on the word niche.  What is the definition of niche?
  7. Very Important (California State Standard of what you need to know before you graduate!!):    Body and beak variation occurs ______________. This means that variation, or changes, in a living thing’s body are not planned.  They result from random changes in D __ __, or m u ___ ___ ___ ___ ___ ___ ___.  (this is not on the web site; you may need to ask me if you do not remember this from first semester)
  8. The birds with the best-suited bodies and beaks for the particular environment survive and pass along the successful ___________ from one generation to another through natural selection.  This is like in our hand, fork, knife, spoon and bean activity, where the most successful trait, or the ______ trait, got passed down from one generation to another.
  9.      a.  After the severe __________ in 1977, vegetation withered and died.  The small, soft __________ were quickly eaten by birds, and the large, tough ________ were the ones left.

    b. Describe which type of birds could eat these seeds and why?

    c.  What happened to the smaller finches?  What does “perished” mean?

10. The big-beaked finches survived and were able to _________________, and the offspring after the 1977 drought tended to have ____________ __________. 

11.  a.  Then, after the rain of 1984-85, there were more of what type of seeds? 

  b.  Therefore, the birds with ___________ _________ were better adapted to eat these types of seeds, and they survived better and produced the most __________.

        A.  8.1 mm            B.  9.4 mm            C.  10.7 mm            D. 12.0 mm

       13.  Look at the second graph.  In 1978, the average beak depth for the birds was

            A.  7.9 mm       B.  9.5 mm       C.  10.1 mm       D.  11.6 mm

       14.  Explain the difference in the average beak depth from 1976 to 1978.

15.  Look at Figure 2 (the third graph).  This graph shows a couple things:

a.  The midparent bill depth and the offspring bill depth are

A.  not related.              B.  directly related.            C.  inversely related.

b.  Are there more dark circles (1978 population) at the bottom of the graph or the top of the graph?  Why?
