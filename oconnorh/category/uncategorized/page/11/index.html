Chemistry Lesson- Graphing sodium thiosulfate + HCl data

March 8, 2012 Copernicus Uncategorized

 

Open Microsoft Excel.

Enter your 7 data points for both drops of Na2S2O3 and 1/Time in 1/seconds. Do NOT enter units in the boxes- just numbers.

Column A:  Drops Na2S2O3;  Column B:  Time (in 1/seconds)

  • Select/Highlight all the data you copied, including the column headings.
  • Pull down the Insert menu; select Chart…
  • Click on XY (Scatter) from the Chart type list (the picture where the dots are connected).  Click Next >.
  • A small picture of your graph should come up.  Click Next >.
  • Type in a Chart title:  “Concentration vs 1/Time – [Type Your Name].”  Type in Value (X) axis:  “Drops of 0.15 M Na2S2O3”  Type in Value (Y) axis:  “1/Time (in seconds)”. Click Next >.
  • Choose “As new sheet:”  Click Finish.
  •  Before you print it out –> go to Chart menu, select Add Trendlines, Click on the correct box (which one?), then Options, then click on the two boxes:  Display equation on chart and display R-squared value on chart.  Click Ok. 
  • Print out with your name on it!!!!

Chemistry Chapter 14 & 15 Study Guide

February 23, 2012 Copernicus Uncategorized

Chapter 14 & 15 Study Guide

1.  Which might be solid at room temperature? H2O, NaCl, PH3, H2

2.  Which might have the lowest boiling point? H20, NaCl, PH3, H2  

3.  Which of the 3 intermolecular forces is the strongest? Weakest?

4.  What is the major attractive force between NaCl? H2? Water?

5.  What phase or phases of water exist at 1 atm & 0°C?

6.  What are the freezing points & boiling points of water in C & K?

7.  Be able to do a problem like Example 14.1 page 446. 

8.  Know the relationship between London forces & molar/atomic mass.

9.  Are molecules closest together in solid? Liquid? Gas?  What about water?

10. Define evaporation.

11.  What is the:

        a.  Relationship between boiling point & vapor pressure

        b.  Relationship between London forces & vapor pressure

        c.  Relationship between temperature & vapor pressure

        d.  Relationship between atmospheric pressure & boiling temperature

12.  Give 3 examples of ionic solids.  Give 2 examples of molecular solids.

13.  Which makes the most ions in solution, silver chloride or sodium phosphate?
14.  Can a gas be a solute?
15.  If I put peas in water, is that a solution?
16.  Define using your textbook: dilute, saturated, unsaturated, 
     Define using dictionary.com:  dissolution, supersaturated, concentrated
17.  How can you make something dissolve faster?
18.  Mass percent – know the formula and be able to do #13a & 13c page 499 
19.  What does a "3 molar solution" mean?
20.  Calculate Molarity if given grams of solute & Liters of water. 
21.  Calculate grams if given Molarity & Liters. 
22.  Calculate Normality if given grams of solute & Liters of solution. Be able to do #44a page 501.
23.  How will boiling point & freezing point be affected by adding a solute?
