Environmental Science – Seed Growth

February 6, 2009 Copernicus Uncategorized

Look at your data table for seed growth.  

Using Excel, enter the data for your seed growth.  

(Remember: DO NOT ENTER ANY LETTERS OR WORDS INTO YOUR DATA TABLE)

Column A:  Day Number;  Column B:  Sprout Length of Seed One;  Column C:  Sprout Length of Seed Two; Column D: Sprout Length of Seed Three, Column E:  Spout Length of Seed Four, etc.

Column F should be the average of your Seed Lengths.  I will help you with this.

Highlight Data.  Select (XY) Scatter.  Choose the graph where the points are connected.  Click Next. Next again.

Chart Title – Seed Growth in pH _____ – Your Name.

(X) Value – Day

(Y) Value – Average of Sprout Length

Click Next.

Click As New Sheet.  Click Finish.

Graphing Mass Extinctions on Earth over Time

1.  Open an Excel Document.

2.  DO NOT ENTER ANY LETTERS OR WORDS.  Enter the numbers for millions of years in Column A; number of families in Column B.

Millions of years:  525, 510, 500, 475, 450, 440, 435, 415, 410, 400, 380, 370, 350, 333, 325, 240, 235, 220, 205, 100, 80, 65, 0

# of Families:  100, 180, 160, 300, 410, 405, 325, 425, 430, 425, 440, 440, 367, 395, 435, 375, 210, 380, 240, 550, 600, 630, 760

3.  Pull down Insert menu.  Select Chart.  Select (XY) Scatter.  Select the graph with connected points.  Click Next.  Click Next (again). 

4.  Chart Title:  Mass Extinctions – Your Name.  Value (X) Axis:  millions of years ago.  Value (Y) Axis:  number of families.  Click Next.
