Chemistry Lesson 10/18/10 – Balancing Equations and Writing Chemical Equations

October 18, 2010 Copernicus Uncategorized

Go to http://richardbowles.tripod.com/chemistry/balance.htm

1.  Chemicals that react (called the _______________) are on the left-hand side, and the chemicals that they produce (called the ______________) are on the right-hand side.

2. Some reactions are _______________, which means that the reactants react together to form the products, but as soon as the products are formed, they start to react together to reform the reactants!  These reactions form reactants and products simultaneously until the reaction reaches ________________.  These reactions do not have a single arrow; they have a ___________   ___________.

3.  Write the balanced chemical reaction for Aluminum metal reacting with Oxygen to form Aluminum Oxide.  Also, draw the atoms to show the conservation of mass. 

4.  If you need to balance a chemical equation, you can change the subscripts….True….False.

5.  What are the four abbreviations in parentheses that represent the three states of matter as well as chemicals in solution.

#6-15.  Copy the ten equations from the bottom of the website and write them in balanced form.  Also, add the (s), (l), (g), and (aq) as described by the equation.

Go to http://members.cox.net/mr.graham/Assignments_files/balancing_equations_worksheet.htm

Do #1-50 only.
