Chemistry Silver Whiskers Lab Questions

November 16, 2012 Copernicus Uncategorized

Chemistry Silver Whiskers Lab Questions:

First, correct your answers from yesterday; then answer any new questions [hints/help are in brackets]:

1.  It is important to coil the copper wire, because it increases the surface area for the reaction to occur.  If the wire had not been coiled, there would have been less of a reaction.

2.  Many reactions, like this one, will not occur unless the reactants are dissolved in water.  If you just mix solid silver nitrate with solid copper, they won’t react.

3.  Yes.  The evidence is that it is a consistent solution; there was no solid silver nitrate at the bottom of the original solution.

4.  Silver.  It looks grey, somewhat shiny, and metallic, just like silver.

5.  ________g /  63.55 g per mole

6.  0.0015 mole AgNO3

8.  Blue.  Light grey.  This change represents a chemical reaction, which are often indicated by color changes.

9.  The purpose of washing off the crystals is to clean off the copper and nitrate ions.  Without cleaning, the mass of the crystals will be inaccurately higher.

10.  [do the math and subtract your final mass of copper wire from the original mass of copper wire].  The decrease in mass shows that some of the ______________  reacted with the _____________   _______________.

11.  _________________ was NOT the limiting reagent, because there was a lot of it left at the end of the reaction.  Therefore, the limiting reagent must have been ______________   ________________.

12.  [(initial copper mass – final copper mass) / 63.55 g per mole]

13.  [(mass of beaker & sliver – mass of beaker only) / atomic mass of silver]

14.  Cu + 2AgNO3 –> 2Ag + Cu(NO3)2          Look at the board:  is this a combustion, synthesis, decomposition, single replacement, double displacement, or acid-base reaction?

15.  Silver crystals.  Copper nitrate solution.

16.  [See # 11 above.  Look at your data table for the # of moles.]

17. [ 0.0015 moles AgNO3 x (MOLE RATIO FROM BALANCED EQUATION) =  ___________  0.0015 moles Ag].

18.  [compare #17 to #13]

19.  [List 3 logical sources of error]

20.  Why do you use the moles of AgNO3 and not moles of Cu in the calculation in #17?  Hint:  it has to do with #11 and #16.

21.  What was your percent yield of silver?  [percent yield is from Chapter 9:     Percent yield = actual yield/theoretical yield x 100%     SO actual yield is from #13 and theoretical yield is from #17.]
