Electron Distribution using A Pencil

Prelab Questions:

  1. Why isn’t it possible to determine the exact path of an electron in an atom? (leave space for answer)
  2. What does quantum theory of the atom tell you about the location of the electron in an atom?
  3. What do the pencil marks represent in this lab?

Procedures:

  1. Take out a piece of paper. On the paper, in the center, make a mark. Measure 1.5 cm , 3cm, 4.5cm, 6cm, and 7.5cm from the mark and mark a mark at each point. Do this for all four directions on the paper, up down, left and right.
  2. Connect the four marks at 1.5cm to make a circle.
  3. Do this with the marks at 3cm, 4.5 cm, 6cm and 7.5cm so that you have 5 concentric rings.
  4. Put the paper on the floor and drop a pencil form table height on to the paper 100 times. ( try to aim for the center dot which represents the nucleus of the atom.)
  5. Pick up the paper and count the dots in each circle and record in data table.

Data Table:

Ring Distance from Nucleus Number of pencil marks in ring
1 3cm  
2 6cm  
3 9cm  
4 12cm  
5 15cm  
Outside the rings Beyond 15cm  

Calculations:

  1. Using the data in your table make a bar graph of the number of pencil marks in each ring. Number of marks Y axis and ring on the X axis.

Analysis:

  1. Using your graph, in which region would you most likely to find electrons? In which region would you be least likely to find electrons?
  2. Were you able to determine the exact path by which the pencil traveled to make the mark? How does this relate to quantum theory?
  3. What would your graph look like if you dropped the pencil 200 times?

Conclusion:

    1. What did you learn?
    2. Why do the pencil drops represent electrons and how does this relate to quantum theory?
    3. Problems?
    4. Improvements?

Genetics Test Topics

November 30, 2010 Copernicus Uncategorized

surface area and volume as a cell grows

cell dividing:  cytokinesis;  nucleus dividing:  mitosis

cell cycle:  G1, S, G2, M and generally what happens in each phase

structure of a chromosome – DNA, proteins (histones).  Coils of DNA and histones = nucleosomes.

phases of mitosis

mitosis:  cell divides and the 2 resulting daughter cells have the same # of chromosomes as before mitosis (in humans, diploid–>diploid); meiosis:  cell divides and the 4 resulting gamete cells (eggs or sperm) have half the # of chromomes (in humans, diploid–>haploid)

cyclins, cancer, tumor

Mendel’s experiments:  P, F1, F2 – what happened in each generation?

Probability:  What is the probability that a human couple will have a female baby?  a male baby?

Two identical alleles:  homozygous;  two different alleles:  heterozygous

Practice Punnett Squares!

How many allele combinations are possible if the pea plant is PpTt?  4!!  PT, Pt, pT, pt

Know codominance, incomplete dominance, and polygenic inheritance.

Crossing Over – what is it and how it happens.

Structure of DNA – double helix, A,G,T,C, sugar-phosphate backbone.  DNA is made of nucleotides.  A nucleotide is a sugar (deoxyribose), a phosphate group, and a nitrogen base (Adenine, Guanine, Cytosine, Thymine).  The amount of A is about equal to the amount of ___, and the amount of G is about equal to the amount of ___.

DNA replication – study the process.

How are DNA and RNA similar?  How are they different?  What is the role of each type of RNA?

Know the processes of transcription and translation (you don’t need to know the enzyme names and about the A and P site, but you do need to know the ribosome, mRNA, codons, transfer RNA, and amino acids).

Know:  definition of a point mutation; lac operon (operons are only in bacteria; they are not in eukaryotes).

Karyotype – what does it show? (homologous pairs, sex chromosomes, autosomes)  what can’t it show?

Male – XY; Female XX.  But what are the sex chromosomes in their gametes (eggs and sperm)?

Pedigrees – what does the symbols represent? 

Multiple Alleles – blood type.

Most sex-linked genes are on the X chromosome (example – colorblindness)

Failure of chromosomes to separate during meiosis is called nondisjunction and it may lead to Down syndrome.
