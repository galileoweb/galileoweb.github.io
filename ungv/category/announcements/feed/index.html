    Due: _________________________________       (30 points)

     

    Create a visual presentation and present to the class.  You must also prepare note cards during your presentation.  If you are doing a Powerpoint presentation, it must have a minimum of six slides (including the title slide).  Presentations length: 4-6 minutes).  All presentations must have an original title.

     

    1. Explain your article to the class (paraphrased).

     

    1. Explain your interest in this article.

     

    1. Why is your news story credible?

     

    1. Explain why this is news.
      1. Timeliness — News that is happening right now, news of interest to readers right now.
      2. Relevance — The story happened nearby or is about a concern of local interest.
      3. Magnitude — The story is great in size or number; for example, a tornado that destroys a couple houses might not make the news but a story about a tornado that devastates a community would be very newsworthy.
      4. Unexpectedness — Something unusual, or something that occurs without warning.
      5. Impact — News that will affect a large number of readers.
      6. Reference to someone famous or important — News about a prominent person or personality.
      7. Oddity — A unique or unusual situation.
      8. Conflict — A major struggle in the news.
      9. Reference to something negative — Bad news often “sells” better than good news.
      10. Continuity — A follow-up or continuation to a story that has been in the news or is familiar.
      11. Emotions — Emotions (such as fear, jealousy, love, or hate) increase interest in a story.
      12. Progress — News of new hope, new achievement, new improvements

     

    1. What is the problem/issue being presented?  What is the greater impact of this story?
    2. What are the solutions proposed (by you)?  What other problems/issues might this solution result in?
    3. How is this article relevant in our lives?
    4. Pose one open-ended question that engages the class in a discussion.

    2. Save onto USB
    3. Cite sources (MLA format)

     

    Posters:

    1. Must be on poster paper
    2. Title/images
    3. Cite sources (MLA format)
