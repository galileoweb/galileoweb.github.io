3/11/09 Meeting Minutes

April 3, 2009 Karin Lau Meeting Minutes

School Site Council Meeting-March 11, 2009
X  Agenda Attached
X  Attendance/sign-in attached
Present:  Vicki Pesek, Ben Chun, Stan DeBella, Doug Page, Jonathan Ring, Bettie Grinnell, Audrey Ricci, Leland Wong, Dominic Tsang, Karin Lau, Jessica Colvin
Absent (Excused):  Mele Lau-Smith, Linda Liu, Kathleen Golata, Jessica Wong, Samantha Lee
X  Minutes of previous meeting approved.

Decision Made/Issues Reviewed

I.  Call to Order
–B. Grinnell, SSC chair, called the meeting to order at 3:35 pm.

II.  Approval of 2/11/2009 Meeting Minutes
–Members had been given an e-copy prior to the meeting.   B. Chun noted 3 corrections on page 3:  Cisco 1142, not Cisco 802; $30,000 to purchase a mobile lab with laptops, not lap books for the science department; $30,000 to purchase a mobile lab with laptops, not lab books for the school.   S. DeBella moved to approve the minutes with the corrections; D. Tsang seconded the motion.  All nine elected members voted to accept the minutes with the corrections.

III.  Wellness Program Update
–The Wellness and the PTSA will co-sponsor a parent workshop entitled “Teen Online:  Learn to be an Internet Savvy Parent and Internet Safety”.  The workshop will take place tonight immediately following the PTSA meeting at 6:00 pm and the workshop at 6:45 pm.  Dinner and translation will be provided.  Lots of publicity has gone out, they are hopeful that the turnout will be well attended.
–The Wellness had added 4 new support groups this month in addition to their current 12 support groups.  The 4 new ones were:  grief counseling by Dana; how to develop social skills for the special education boys; substance abuse for 9th grade girls; 9th and 10th grade shy boys by Dr. Leong.
–Dana created a Wellness web site for the students.  There are games to help students navigate the site and prizes are awarded, which are provided by the PTSA.

IV.  Principal’s Report
–V. Pesek had gotten her BSC feedback from the District.  When the panel evaluated the BSC, they used “yellow” to indicate outstanding areas and “pink” for ambiguous ones.  Most of the comments were in “yellow”, but there were several “pink” areas, which required additional clarification.  For example, Galileo had hired Susan English-James as a consultant to be an intervention specialist to work with at-risk students.  The District questioned her qualifications as compared to a certificated staff.  Another area was how the school could improve on its community meetings, to make them better attended?  Should the meetings have a theme to draw people to them?

V.  Old Business
1.  Balanced Scorecard Feedback from t    he District
–Please refer to the Principal’s Report section.

1a.  Balanced Scorecard Comments from Members
–M. Lau was absent but she had given her comments to K. Lau to turn in for her.  Z. Duskin will incorporate all the comments from parents and students and submit the final version on April 17th, 2009.

2.  SCE Budget:  Technology Update
–B. Chun and M. Bush were doing their best to meet the purchasing deadline.  The school had begun to receive the orders.  This huge tech fund would support the equipment update of the school.  At the end, the school will have some great wireless network, might be one of the best in the district.
–M. Bush had a lot of logistics to consider for her projector purchases.  At the CUE conference she had attended, she had received more information and more quotes for the installation.  At this juncture, the school would purchase carts for these projectors rather than installing them on the ceiling.
–The District’s IT department is in the process of upgrading their equipment and network.  They will work on it over the Spring Break.

VI.  New Business
1.  SCE Budget:  Curriculum Development/Conferences, the Selection Process
–Z. Duskin gave the members a copy of the handout, which she had developed:  Curriculum Alignment Professional Development Process Outline.  The outline and other information could be accessed on her web site.  The handout delineated the professional development’s goals, background, process, end products, and compensation.  Teachers needed to fill out the application, which is on the backside.  Z. Duskin and V. Pesek will decide who gets to attend; the priority goes to people from different departments.  The on-line option suits the teachers who have had the Understanding By Design training.  Novice teachers to the concept should attend the conference in order to get the exposure.  Z. Duskin thus far has received one paper application though ten teachers have expressed interest.
–Teachers will get a whole week’s extended hours pay to develop a unit for their classes.

2. Extended Calendar Request Guideline
–Teachers, who work beyond the school days, are eligible to receive extended hours to compensate for their time. And when they do that, they ought to get pre-approval for their extended hours pay from the administration.  In order to be equitable, some guidelines should be established.  Members suggested that when a teacher is assigned to a project/task, which requires a substantial amount of time, which has funding viability, and/or which is beyond the scope of the curriculum, then the teacher should be compensated, i.e. tutors, athletic directors, band directors, project managers, etc.  D. Tsang suggested that these Jefferson award recipients should get some cash rewards.
–D. Page proposed to put the list on-line on a Google document to make the list more transparent.

3.  Preliminary Budget-Due Date:  April 2, 2009
–K. Lau passed out the green sheet:  Galileo HS Preliminary Allocations for SY 2009/2010.  Galileo received $9,105,050, which was $81,028 less than the Fall Final Allocations.  The allocations included the mayor’s Rainy Day Fund and were based on 2,123 projected enrollees.  Galileo did not receive the “Hard to Fill” school bonuses.  Galileo will have to pay for a PE teacher’s salary, which is currently paid for by the District.
–In order to submit a balanced preliminary budget, the school had to ask the School-to-Career office to pick up more FTEs (full-time-equivalences).  The priority is to preserve all of the positions, then the remaining money can be allocated to the non-personnel categories.  Mr. Borrelli will go back to .60 next year; the SCE money paid for his increased .40 FTEs in the spring semester.
–The members debated weather to hold an on-line discussion or have an emergency meeting to review and approve the preliminary budget.  The consensus is to have a meeting, which will be on Wednesday, March 25, 2009 at 3:20 pm.

VII.  Open Forum
–The District will move Health Ed and Career Ed to the 9th grade.  Ms. Ramos is willing to go back to school so she can teach these classes.
–The District will no longer offer Driver Ed because it is no longer a graduation requirement.  Students may take it at City College or on-line.  Mr. Elizarde, who has taught Driver Ed for the past 20+ years, will teach Career Ed.
–The District is considering eliminating the US Government course in the 12th grade and making it as a part of the US History curriculum.

–S. DeBella moved to adjourn the meeting; B. Grinnell seconded the motion, and the meeting was adjourned at 4:40 pm.

Minutes taken and prepared by Karin Lau_____________________________
