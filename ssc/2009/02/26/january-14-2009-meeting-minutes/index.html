February 26, 2009 Karin Lau Meeting Minutes

San Francisco Unified School District
Galileo Academy of Science and Technology
1150 Francisco Street
San Francisco, CA  94109

Room 210 at 1:20 P.M.
Location and Time of Meeting

School Site Council Meeting-January 14, 2009
X  Agenda Attached
X  Attendance/sign-in attached
Present:  Vicki Pesek, Ben Chun, Stan DeBella, Doug Page, Bettie Grinnell, Mele Lau-Smith, Leland Wong, Linda Liu, Dominic Tsang, Jessica Wong, Samantha Lee, Karin Lau, Jessica Colvin; Absent (Excused):  Jonathan Ring, Audrey Ricci, Kathleen Golata
X  Minutes of previous meeting approved.

Decision Made/Issues Reviewed

I.  Call to Order
–B. Grinnell, SSC chair, called the meeting to order at 1:32 pm.

II.  Approval of 12/10/2008 Meeting Minutes
–Members had been given an e-copy prior to the meeting, but majority of them still preferred receiving a hard copy at the meeting.   S. DeBella moved to approve the minutes; B. Grinnell seconded the motion.  All nine elected members voted to accept the minutes as written.

III.  Principal’s Report
–Galileo was recognized as one of the exemplary schools for having excellent School Site Council record keeping.
–The Board of Ed voted to have open enrollment for all the honors and AP classes starting the spring semester of 2009.  Many students weren’t taking advantage of the new policy because they didn’t want to make any major changes in their course schedules.
–This open enrollment policy would be more difficult for the science department to implement.  Certain prerequisite skills were mandatory in some courses; therefore, making it challenging for the department to accommodate this new policy.
–Two new Algebra 1 classes would be created in the spring semester to accommodate students who will fail the course in the fall; J. Ring and M. Meeks will be the teachers.
–Currently, students make up their failed classes by going to summer school, night school and/or Cyber High, because the master schedule is set up to offer the odd numbered courses in the fall and the even numbered ones in the spring.   Soon the master schedule will offer all numbered courses within a semester; therefore, enabling students to make up or get caught up within that period.  Site council members were concerned about the availability of teachers to cover all sequenced courses within a semester.  V. Pesek found it more challenging to create a well-balanced master schedule but it was doable.  What she had to do was to consolidate some low-enrolled classes to create new ones, and so forth.
–Galileo would receive 13 new students from Newcomer for the spring semester.  The bulk of the Newcomer students had been assigned to Lincoln High School.  They will come to Galileo to be tested on Thursday, January 15.
–V. Pesek was considering projecting next year’s enrollment at 2,000, but with attrition, she might up the number to 2,150.  Teachers tended to like the smaller class sizes and lower enrollment; therefore the school is less crowded in the hallways, and its teachers having their own classrooms, etc.

IV.  Old Business
1.  Balanced Scorecard/Strategic Plan
–On the professional development day (1/20/2009), teachers and staff would participate in the development of the BSC, which is due on January 30, 2009.
–The administration did a decent job of communicating with the faculty, staff, and parents.  They needed to work on involving the students more.
–Part of the BSC is that students will be able to earn variable credits. For example, if a student passes the first marking period, but fails the second and the third, the student will be able to earn 1 to 2 credits instead of zero for the entire semester.

2.  07/08 EIA/SCE Carryover Budget
–Jorge Cuevas Antillon, supervisor of the Department of the State & Federal Programs, has been coaching and advising the Galileo team on how to spend this carryover windfall within the programs’ guideline.  The money couldn’t be used to supplant the district’s financial responsibility.  The money should be used to support the Educational Disadvantaged Youth (EDY) students and to transform teaching so the target population would be engaged and achieve success.  Items such as installing wireless network and compensating teachers for professional development and curriculum development were within the programs’ guidance.  Items such as asset tracking and installing electrical outlets weren’t allowable.  Justifications for these expenditures needed to be reflected in the current year’s Academic Plan.
–The school must zero out this fund or the money gets rolled back into the general bucket and gets redistributed to all the other schools next year.
–If Galileo Academy is found out of compliance, the District will be penalized and may need to pay the Department of State and Federal Programs back.
–The SSC needs to approve this budget proposal.  Instead of waiting for the next meeting on 2/11/09 to take place, K. Lau can email the members the budget spreadsheet for their perusal to get the momentum going.

V.  New Business
–The SSC made the recommendation to project next year’s enrollment at 2,150.  With attrition, the actual enrollment should level off to 2,000, which makes it more manageable.
–L. Hughes will truly retire; therefore, the school can allocate her .20 FTE to fund someone else.
–The school should vote on next year’s passing minutes; to keep them at 7 or revert back to 5.  The members prefer the periods to end in a round number, which is easier to remember.

VI.  Open Forum
–The system to record student attendance is antiquated.  The District may not have the resources or the manpower to upgrade it.  If the District has to outsource the project, it is not cost effective.

–S. DeBella moved to adjourn the meeting; B. Grinnell seconded the motion, and the meeting was adjourned at 2:32 pm.

Minutes taken and prepared by Karin Lau_____________________________
