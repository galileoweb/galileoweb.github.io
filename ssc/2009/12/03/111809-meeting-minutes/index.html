School Site Council Meeting-November 18, 2009

X  Attendance/sign-in attached

Present:  Stan DeBella, Doug Page, Bettie Grinnell, Mele Lau-Smith, Audrey Ricci, Leland Wong, Linda Liu, Karin Lau, Zoe Duskin

Absent (Excused): Vicki Pesek, Ben Chun, Kathleen Golata, Monica Eng, Mark Huang

X  Minutes of the previous meetings approved

Decision Made/Issues Reviewed

I.  Call to Order

–B. Grinnell, SSC chair, called the meeting to order at 3:33 pm.  There were seven elected members present; a quorum was established.  The chair welcomed everyone at the meeting.  L. Wong moved to approve the minutes as written.  D. Page seconded the motion, and all voted in favor of approving the minutes.

II.  Old Business

1.  Balanced Scorecard (BSC) Updates

– The BSC as a working document will continue to be edited to reflect the current condition and needs of the school.  The BSC is aligned with the WASC Review Report.  The WASC Visitation will occur in the spring of 2011; therefore, the administration will begin writing the report in the spring of 2010.  The best way to comprehend the workings of the WASC review process is to get trained on it and become a reviewer.  N. Lambert and B. Chun have gone on the training and K. Golata has applied and is waiting for approval. The training, which is paid for by WASC, lasts 3 days, and consists of visiting a school and writing a report about the visit.

III.  New Business

1.  School Programs Updates

– Galileo held an Open House on Monday, November 16 in the evening for prospective families, whose children go to public and parochial schools.  There were 103 families in attendance.  One of the objectives for this event was the intended stepped-up effort in reaching out to the African-American and Latino families.  Their enrollment has been in the decline for the past several years, especially the AA group, whose enrollment has plummeted to 5.6% this year, which is the lowest percentage rate in the past 30 years.  The AA population in the District is 11%; therefore, Galileo has a skew representation of this ethnic group at 5.6%.  One factor for this decline could be that many AA families have moved out of the city and the remaining families have chosen to attend other high schools, i.e. Lincoln, Washington, over Galileo.  A member wondered if the Asian population at Galileo surpasses the 40% cut-off rate.

– The Board of Ed has passed a resolution to implement the Restorative Justice Approach to reduce suspension rates in all the schools.  Z. Duskin has been working closely with Student Court to look at the underlying causes why the students commit the infractions, rather than the punishments doled out to them.  Next, she will work with the deans and then the counselors to get them in sync with the practice.  A member questioned why the Deans were not the first ones to receive in-service.  Though the deans are not on board yet with the model, the school has always supported their decisions.  The Student Court will continue to handle the low level infractions.  Participants gain positive learning experience from their involvement with Student Court.

– Teachers have begun submitting proposals on how to earn the Prop A professional development hours.  Teachers will get two hours for attending the 8th Grade Open House.

– The Italian Consulate is willing to give Galileo a six to eight thousand dollars grant to implement an Italian language program.  The staff will work with the Credentialing Office to streamline the credentialing process should the need arise.  But the administration feels that the demand for Japanese and Arabic surpasses the need for Italian.

IV.  Open Forum

– The Class of 1942 donated the two marble benches located at the Francisco gate entrance, to the school and stone lions in the courtyard at the entrance to the Polk Street building. The benches had deteriorated over the years, but the PTSA has stepped in to restore their condition.  The Class of 1942 is designing a plaque that will commemorate their donation.

S. DeBella moved to adjourn the meeting.  A. Ricci seconded the motion and the meeting was adjourned at 4:00 pm.

Minutes taken and prepared by Karin Lau
