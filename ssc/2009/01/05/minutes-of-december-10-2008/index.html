12/10/08 Meeting Minutes

January 5, 2009 machtayh Meeting Minutes

I.  Welcome/Introductions

–K. Lau called the meeting to order at 3:35pm   Introductions were conducted around the room.

II.  New Business

1.  Selection of SSC Chairperson/Roles of SSC and Chairperson

–Members were given the pertinent handout to peruse.  D.Page nominated B. Grinnell as the chairperson; the group of student reps would be the co-chairpersons.  Members unanimously voted to accept both nominations.

2.  Balanced Scorecard (BSC) and Strategic Plan

–Strategic Plan:  plan for equity

–BSC:  A metric to measure students’ achievement; hence, providing schools data to work toward closing the achievement gap, and to map out projection for the next several years, accomplish this objective by differentiating instruction for students.  Galileo is ahead of the game compare to others.

–The objectives of these documents were for schools to map out their strategies to ensure equity and equal access among ALL students and to meet their needs.  These plans of actions had an initial due date on January 20, 2009.  Thus far, the District hadn’t created a template for the schools.

–The superintendent had proposed some mandates, and if they were approved by the Board of Education, would affect all the schools with varying timeframes.  One of the mandates was for all the subjects to carry variable 5 credits.  For example, if a student received a “C” in the 1st quarter, failed the 2nd and 3rd quarters, then the student would be able to earn 2 credits for his 1st quarter’s work.  Whereas under the traditional grading system, the student would receive an F for the semester, thus, earning no credit at all.  The school then had the opportunity to help the student make up the other 3 credits either through Cyber High, evening schools, or other means, so the student could fulfill the graduation requirement.  English departments, in general, were opposed to the proposal, citing that any non-conventional instructions would not be equivalent to their conventional counterparts.

–Another mandate starting with Class of 2014 would be for all the SFUSD’s high school graduation requirements to be equivalent to the UC’s and CSU’s a to g admissions requirements; thus, making all the high school graduates college eligible.  To further support his “Equity and Equal Access for All” objective, all the Honors and AP courses would have an open-enrollment policy, which meant that no assessments would be imposed, as long as parents and students were willing to meet the expectations of the courses.

–D. Page commented that many of the math classes required some pre-requisites.  Therefore, as long as the students met these pre-requisites, they should be qualified for entering these Honors/AP courses.  He also commented that some math courses could be taken out of sequence.

–S. DeBella cited that Chemistry was sequential, but Biology was not; therefore, making some classes more flexible for this new policy than the others.

–The Master Schedule had to be redone every semester to accommodate all these changes and to meet these new mandates.

–In order for students to complete Calculus before they graduate, they needed to take Algebra 1 and 2 in the 8th grade.  So when they entered high school, they would be placed in Geometry.  The current policy had students assessed and if they got a “4” or “5” on the assessment test, they would then be placed in Geometry.  The new mandate would cause some of these 9th grade Geometry classes to be heterogeneous.  One recommendation was to rename these 9th grade Geometry classes differently.

–The instructional calendar might be modified, which would make the fall semester go from mid-August to December and the spring semester from early January to late May.  This modified calendar would have the same number of instructional days.  But over the transitional year, teachers would lose 2 weeks of vacation time.  Summer school would be 4 weeks long going from 8 am to 4 pm, full days.   It was suggested to compensate teachers for these lost 2 weeks of vacation time.  Union reps and the District should negotiate.

3.  SSC Summit

–The Summit took place on a Saturday.  There were a whole bunch of workshops for SSC members to learn the workings of the school site council and what the BSC was really about.  N. Lambert presented; P. Van Court attended; and B. Chun also attended.

4.  Tech Plan Review

–M. Chiu facilitated the rediscovery of $624,000 in federal money for Galileo’s tech plan, which had a caveat of having the fund spent by the end of the school year.  Immediately, Ben had put together a proposal extrapolating ideas from the site’ ongoing Tech Strategy Plan and went with V. Pesek to present it to Brianne Ford, who was the head of the Information Technology (IT) Department.

–With this one-time windfall, it would be in the best interest of Galileo to create a long-range plan to get the best use of the money.  One way, the school must maintain some kind of asset tracking/management, and also perform an accurate fiscal calculation by taking a look at the site’s current inventory.  It was vital to look at the ratio of computer usage by teacher to teacher/teacher to students.  Once we had the information, the school would ensure that every teacher had a laptop, and every classroom an interactive whiteboard. Also, part of the Tech Plan was to train teachers on integrating technology in their instructions in the classrooms and to provide continual support.  If the school were to have wireless internet access on campus, security would be an issue.  The school needed to upgrade the infrastructure and core network. In a normal year, the cost of these ongoing replacements would usually be larger than the annual budget allocation.  To compensate the deficit, the school could do fund raising, grant writing, etc…

–A pivotal goal of the plan was for each department to have their own computer lab.  But because space was at a premium, some labs would have to be mobile.

–D. Page commented that teachers on staff lacked the technical expertise to do asset management but suggested Carol Ingram, who was  knowledgeable & capable of handling asset tracking.

–B. Ford and her IT Department had parcel tax and Prop H moneys to support this endeavor.

–A pilot program might be proposed that teachers, who received training, would be placed in a group who would have a wider range of useablilty.

–Asset management included taking inventories to determine the age of the equipment, the replacement costs, thief, etc….  A. Ricci had the experience with asset management, so she would help with these efforts.

–Plans to use the $25,000 gift from Doug Piper had changed since this windfall surfaced.  Some expenditure had already been incurred, but because the gift had no time limit, Galileo had the luxury of time to mull things over.  Yet, the school still needed to strategize on how this money should be allocated.

–Since the windfall came from the State Compensatory Education (SCE) program, the money spent needed to be in compliance.  Some questions to keep in mind….how should teachers and classes support this windfall in technology?  When resources in the district decline, how could the school continue to support teachers in this area.

III.  Open Forum

–A member asked if having wifi for students, like Lowell, was possible.  Right now what Galileo had didn’t support it, but, hopefully with this windfall, any update would improve the situation.  Though one thing to bear in mind was the liability for students

–A member queried if the school could support the drum practice, but at a discreet location, where the noises wouldn’t disturb people.  The school had tried putting the Drum Corps at all different locations, but with the same results; wherever they went, people found them too noisy.  The problem would end soon because they were practicing for their upcoming competition.

D. Page moved to adjourn the meeting; J. Ring seconded the motion, and the meeting was adjourned at 5:25 pm.
