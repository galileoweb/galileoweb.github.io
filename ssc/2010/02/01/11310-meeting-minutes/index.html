February 1, 2010 Karin Lau Meeting Minutes

SAN FRANCISCO UNIFIED SCHOOL DISTRICT

Galileo Academy of Science and Technology

1150 Francisco Street, San Francisco, CA  94109

Room 210 at 1:30 P.M.

Location and Time of Meeting

School Site Council Meeting-January 13, 2010

X  Agenda Attached

X  Attendance/sign-in attached

Present:  Vicki Pesek, Ben Chun, Doug Page, Bettie Grinnell, Leland Wong, Monica Eng, Mark Huang, Linda Liu, Karin Lau, Zoe Duskin, Nancy Lambert, Katie Pringle

Absent (Excused): Stan DeBella, Kathleen Golata, Mele Lau-Smith, Audrey Ricci

X  Minutes of the previous meetings approved

Decision Made/Issues Reviewed

I.  Call to Order

–B. Grinnell, SSC chair, called the meeting to order at 1:43 pm.  There were eight elected members present; a quorum was established.  The chair welcomed everyone to the meeting.

II.  Approval of 11/18/09 Meeting

-B. Chun corrected the name of the recycling company from Golden Gate Disposable Company to Golden Gate Disposal in part V, #3.  L. Liu moved to approve the minutes as amended.  B. Chun seconded the motion, and all voted in favor of approving the minutes as amended.

III.  Old Business

1.  School Programs Updates

– The academy and pathway teachers will begin visiting classrooms to promote their programs.  Additionally, there will be an academy/pathway fair to be held on January 28 at lunch for students to learn more about these unique programs.

-Counselors will give grade-level assemblies to inform students about their course selections and the process.  For parent involvement, counselors will be the panel presenters at the PTSA workshop titled “Road to College” at 6:30 pm, which is after the PTSA general meeting at 6:00, on Wednesday, January 13.

-The Community Educational Services (CES) funds 3 full-time positions this school year.  Hopefully, they will continue paying for them, so the programs can be sustained next year.

-The Career Technical Education (CTE) funds 2.8 full-time positions this school year.  Patricia Theel from the CTE office will have a meeting with V. Pesek on Friday to go over budgetary and program concerns and issues.

2.  WASC Accreditation Process Updates

-Z. Duskin has begun the planning process.  She has already met with the leadership team to affirm their leadership roles in this WASC accreditation process.

A big part of the professional development day on Friday, January 29 will be devoted to WASC.  Faculty and staff members will learn what their involvement is and how they can support the process.

-The student and parent committees will convene in the spring of 2010; though any senior committee members will have graduated and not be around for the actual accreditation visit.

-B. Chun questioned the role of the site council in the WASC process, which was delineated as a sub-category (Governance) in the first of the five WASC/CDE Focus on Learning Schoolwide Criteria (Organization).  Z. Duskin ascertained that the site council would have ample opportunity to be involved in a Focus Group.

IV.  New Business

1.  SCE Funded Classroom Technology Updates

-V. Pesek and Z. Duskin created a chart, which was passed out to the council, showing the staff roles and responsibilities of the technology integration program for the spring semester.  M. Bush was not a part of this program because of her added responsibilities in the classroom.  The program involved four people with each having a unique role to make this program effective.

1.  G. Carter, 2 periods-lab supervision, school loop help and coaching, receiving orders, one-on-one coaching, help with basic Microsoft Office Applications;

2.  B. Chun, 2 periods-school wide wireless maintenance, whole school tech projects, instructional technology integration, advise on purchasing decisions, act as a system administrator for School Loop;

3.  T. Ly, full day-technology maintenance, teach/student account management, delivery and initial set up of technology purchases; and

4.  Z. Duskin, assistant principal-manage training and professional development, oversee and ensure distribution of purchases, make final purchasing decisions, to be confirmed by V. Pesek and brought before the SSC, manage computer sign-ups, act as a system administrator for School Loop.

-There was a surplus of equipment in the inventory because of unclaimed items due to some teachers’ units still in working condition and teachers worrying about security issues.

-Security has always been an issue.  Often time items get stolen over the summer, when the campus is wide open for summer school and custodial cleanups.

Items also get stolen out of flimsy cabinets that can easily be broken into, and due to teachers’ lax attitude about safeguarding their belongings.

-To prevent thefts, teachers need to be mindful of these preventive measures: have an acute awareness of the surroundings, secure their belongings whenever possible, lock doors, do not give passwords and school keys to students, do not leave school keys laying around, etc.

2.  Recycling and Composting Regulations/Updates

-K. Pringle has met with the SEU for ideas, which mainly comprise of student driven programs.  Currently, there isn’t a comprehensive recycling program in place at Galileo.  Teachers, who want to implement a recycling program, must come up with their own plans.  The school could support such effort by making the blue recycling bins more accessible and available.

-K. Pringle has also solicited ideas and support from the Earth Club and the Department of Environment about creating a composting program.  The Earth Club could come out and present an assembly to educate students about the concept.  She has also approached George, cafeteria manager, and EiEi, coordinator for the ESLI program about implementing a composting program in the school cafeteria.  So far, EiEi hasn’t got back to her.

-Many district schools such as Balboa, Mission, and Burton had composting programs in place and all had been spearheaded and run by students.  A member suggested that the Nature Club sponsored by Mr. Chinn could be tapped to start up such a program.  It would become a service-learning project for the club members.  And if the students did the collecting of bins from classrooms and depositing the matters into the big collection bin, they could eliminate involving the custodial staff.  The success of the program is depended on the dedication of the students; they can definitely make it work.

-K. Pringle has researched grant opportunities offered by PG&E in the amount of $10,000 per award.  The grant’s objective is to educate the communities about the benefits of solar panels by having a few of them installed onto our roof, which would not be powerful enough to reduce our utility bill.  Some classes are learning about renewable energy; maybe they can provide some support to this project when the time comes.

3.  Calendaring Approved Budget Items for Review

-We will table this item to the next site council meeting on February 10.  M. Lau-Smith was the author of the chart; she had reviewed this term’s meeting minutes thus far and compiled into these categories:  Meeting Date, Decision/Recommendation Considered, Key Consideration(s), Point Person(s), Approved/Recommend, Follow-up.  The site council appreciated all the time and effort went into the task.  We will discuss the chart in details when M. Lau-Smith is present.  One item needed to be mentioned now and is listed in the chart as well is what criteria are used to determine which teachers and staff are qualified to get extended hourly pay.  At the moment, the criteria, if they exist, are ambiguous; thus, allowing some teachers to get paid and the others not. The site council suggested that a set of criteria be formularized so the opportunities for extended pay are equitable and not so arcane.  V. Pesek handed out the blue form-Extended Calendar Request-for the site council to review and comment.  V. Pesek will work with K. Lau to come up with some guidelines and present them to the site council in February.  These guidelines can then be applied to the few people, who have requested extended hourly pay, i.e., J. Taylor, E. Wing, Lisa Franzen, etc.

-The school is still able to pay some teachers for their extended services, but other areas have to be cut in order to survive the school year.  The school will no longer be viable to provide incentives to students for taking the STAR test in April, nor can it continue to give each student a student planner next school year.  The student reps feel that that is the least impacted item to be eliminated, because based on their observation, less than 50% of the student body uses it.  They feel that many of their peers use the cell phones to record dates and things.  M. Eng also relies on School Loop for assignment updates and for progress grades, etc.  Unfortunately, only 20% of teachers regularly update on School Loop and only 50% record their class grades there.  School Loop doesn’t have class attendance as a feature; otherwise, it may encourage more teachers to use it.

-It would be the goal of the school to have 100% of teachers using School Loop, but the transition will take time.  The district hasn’t mandated a 100% participation rate yet, because the superintendent feels that not all teachers have access to a computer.  At Balboa, 75% of teachers regularly update on School Loop and 100% of them will transition over to School Loop to post their grades starting the spring semester.

V.  Open Forum

-The District has been talking about ways to make up for the budget shortfall for next school year.  They are looking at eliminating the advanced placement prep periods, not replacing retired teachers, increasing class sizes, etc.

L. Wong moved to adjourn the meeting.  L. Liu seconded the motion and the meeting was adjourned at 3:01 pm.

Minutes taken and prepared by Karin Lau
