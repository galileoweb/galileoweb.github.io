October 4, 2010 Karin Lau Meeting Minutes

SAN FRANCISCO UNIFIED SCHOOL DISTRICT

Galileo Academy of Science and Technology

1150 Francisco Street, San Francisco, CA  94109

Room 210 at 3:30 P.M.

Location and Time of Meeting

School Site Council Meeting-May 12, 2010

X  Agenda Attached

X  Attendance/sign-in attached

Present: Vicki Pesek, Ben Chun, Stan DeBella, Doug Page, Bettie Grinnell, Mele Lau-Smith, Audrey Ricci, Leland Wong, Linda Liu, Jennifer Zhang, Lisa Frazen, Karin Lau

Absent (Excused): Kathleen Golata, Mark Huang

X  Minutes of the previous meeting pending approval

Decision Made/Issues Reviewed

I.  Call to Order

– B. Grinnell, SSC chair, called the meeting to order at 3:43 pm.  There were ten elected members present; a quorum was established.  The chair welcomed everyone to the meeting.

II.  Approval of 3/24/2010 and 4/14/2010 Meeting Minutes

– B. Chun moved to approve both meeting minutes as presented.  M. Lau-Smith seconded the motion, and all voted in favor of approving both meeting minutes.

III.  Old Business

1.  WASC Accreditation Process Updates

– Z. Duskin was absent; she will give her updates at the next meeting.

2.  School Program Updates

– V. Pesek informed the council that sixteen Galileo teachers had received pink slips and seven of them would actually receive layoff notices.  At the same time, the teacher union, UESF, had been in contractual negotiation with the District.  One of the deals was for the District to reduce the budget-related layoffs from around 350 to less than 200.  If and when that deal materializes, these seven layoffs would be rescinded.

– It was confirmed that the District would pay for 50% of the college and career counselor.  If Galileo receives more funding in the fall, the school would fund the other 50% and make it a full time position.

– The Army stipulated that the school must fund 50% of Sergeant Hardee’s position and the Army would fund the other 50%.  The school has to mull this requirement over before making a final decision.

– So far the enrollment numbers look promising, and it was only based on round two of the enrollment process.  By the end of the cycle, the incoming class should be filled up.

– N. Lambert, assistant principal of student services, will meet with the department heads to go over their respective departments’ singletons and how those singletons impact the master schedule.

– The City of San Francisco might lay off 500 of their city workers.  All of our consolidated clerical staff could get another assignment either in the district or city offices.

– Galileo’s Academic Performance Index (API) went up 17 points to 759.

3.  Environmental Service Learning

– K. Pringle was absent.  L. Franzen would give the updates on behalf of K. Pringle.  Please refer to the next agenda item.

Page 1

IV.  New Business

1.  Garden Project

– A bond measure was passed to make green schoolyard in all the middle and high schools.  The perimeters are wide to encourage more schools to submit their project proposals.  Galileo is interested, but the only possible green space is the rooftop, which measures 165’ long and 40’ wide; and is structurally sound because the roof it sits on is made of concrete.  The space was marked as a garden in the original blueprint and was used at one time to house animals including rabbits, snakes, chickens, and baby chicks, which went home with faculty and staff on the weekends.  In the old days when Norm Keeve was a science teacher, the animal coop was real and was put to good use.

– If a roof garden became a reality, it could be used as an outdoor classroom on a nice day, though it tends to get windy.  The garden food could be used as ingredients for a salad bar.  The special education classes are interested in making the gardening concept into a career education class for their students.

– L. Frazen will author the grant application for an organic garden.  The application had to be concise and detailed.  When the time comes, the grant committee might come and survey the space and the school’s commitment to sustain the garden.  L. Frazen will be prepared; she will bring in some wood chips to make the ground area beautiful and attractive.

2.  Recycling and Composting

– The recycling and composting concepts are intertwined as one for the grant application process.  Before the school received any green bins, the agency mandated that a school wide assembly be held to educate the students on the proper way of composting.  Custodians are not contracted to handle any recycling and composting so the teachers and students have to be the ones to manage the collection.  The school could conduct a survey to see which teachers are wiling to have the composting program implemented in their classrooms.

IV.  Open Forum

– K. Lau passed out the Open House event program, which will be held at 6:00 pm today.

– The baseball championship game against Washington HS will start right after the current Giants game’s conclusion, which will be after 4 pm.  The game is free, but attendees must enter through a special side entrance.

– The school elevators break down easily.  Though one of the two on campus was working yesterday, it did not work today.

L. Liu moved to adjourn the meeting.  M. Lau-Smith seconded the motion and the meeting was adjourned at 4:25 pm.

Minutes taken and prepared by Karin Lau
