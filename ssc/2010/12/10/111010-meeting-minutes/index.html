December 10, 2010 Karin Lau Meeting Minutes

SAN FRANCISCO UNIFIED SCHOOL DISTRICT

Galileo Academy of Science and Technology

1150 Francisco Street, San Francisco, CA  94109

Room 210 at 3:30 P.M.

Location and Time of Meeting

School Site Council Meeting-November 10, 2010

X  Agenda Attached

X  Attendance/sign-in attached

Present: Vicki Pesek, Doug Page, Jonathan Ring, Woodraw Smith, David Zeeman, Patrick Delaney, Mele Lau-Smith, Claire Siegel, Diane Lee, Frank Wu, Michelle Zhang, Karin Lau Katie Pringle

Absent (Excused): Nicole Torres

X  Minutes of the previous meeting approved

Decision Made/Issues Reviewed

I.  Call to Order

– K. Lau called the meeting to order at 3:40 pm.  There were eleven elected members present; a quorum was established.  V. Pesek, principal, welcomed everyone to the meeting, which is the first of the newly elected 2-year term.  Introductions were conducted around the room.

II.  New Business

1.  WASC Update

– Copies of the WASC 2011 Draft Report, which consisted of the first two chapters, were disseminated to the council members.  This report is for the members’ information only and to let them know where the progress of the WASC process is at this juncture.

2.  School Site Council Bylaws

– Copies of the Bylaws were disseminated to the council members to review and comment.  If they deem any necessary changes to the document, they can bring them up at the December 8th meeting.

*Action Item:  Update the SSC Bylaws

3.  Roles of SSC and Chairperson

– The handout delineating the roles of the school site council, chairperson, and co-chairperson was given to the members to review.  P. Delaney nominated D. Zeeman to chair the council, but he gently declined the nomination.  D. Page moved to nominate M. Lau-Smith to be the chairperson, who accepted the nomination.  D. Zeeman seconded the motion.  All voted in favor of the motion and the motion was passed unanimously.

4.  Future Meeting Dates/Time

– In keeping with the past tradition, all future meetings will be held on the second Wednesday of every month at 3:30 pm:  December 8, January 12, February 9, March 9, April 13, May 11.

III.  Open Forum

1.  M. Lau-Smith distributed the chart she had created:  “Galileo Site Council Approval and Review”, which listed the following items for all the meetings held in 2009/2010: (1) date, (2) decision/recommendation considered, (3) key consideration(s), (4) point person(s), (5) approved/recommend, (6) follow-up.  She asked each member to review the chart to get familiarized with the discussions at all the 2009/2010 meetings.

2.  K. Pringle handed out four budget worksheets:

i.  Fall Final School Site Budget/budget Summary for 2010/2011-A list of funding sources* broken down by the allocated categories**.

ii.  Budget Allocations-A funding comparison in all the different funding sources* between the two school years.

iii.  Non-Personnel Allocations-Funding allocated in the different categories for 2010/2011 (classroom instructional materials, other supplies, other equipment, printing, postage, travel/conference, rent/lease equipment, repairs/maintenance, consultant fees, student field trips, other services)

iv.  Staff Allocations-A comparison between the two school years by categories- administrators, counselors, deans, librarian, nurse, paraprofessionals, secretaries, IT tech, and teachers are by departments.

– Members made suggestions to make these handouts more user friendly.  K. Pringle will update them to include the enrollment numbers and to convert the cuts from dollar amounts into percentages.

3.  M. Lau-Smith distributed five budget handouts, which she had summarized from the past several SSC meetings:

i.  Budget Summary-An overview of all the funding sources* broken down by the different categories** for 2009/2010 and 2010/2011.

* General Fund/Weighted Student Formula (WSF), Targeted Instructional, Special Ed/WSF, Economic Impact Aid/State Compensatory Education (EIA/SCE), EIA/ Limited English Proficient (LEP), Prop H PE, Prop H Arts, To Be Specified (TBS).

** Allocated, Encumbered, Unencumbered, Full Time Equivalence (FTE), Salary, Benefits, Extended Services, Non Personnel.

ii.  Budget Allocations-A funding comparison between the two school years in all the different funding sources.

iii.  Non-Personnel Allocations-Funding allocated in the different categories for 2010/2011 (classroom instructional materials, other supplies, other equipment, printing, postage, travel/conference, rent/lease equipment, repairs/maintenance, consultant fees, student field trips, other services)

iv.  Extended Services-Funding allocated in the different categories for 2010/2011 (substitute days, certificated extended hours, instructional aides extra hours)

v.  Staff Allocations-A comparison between the two school years by categories (administrators, counselors, deans, librarian, nurse, paraprofessionals, secretaries, IT tech) and teachers are by departments.

– These two sets of worksheets were very similar in their details and data, which supported the dire budget situations at Galileo.  Galileo got their budget cut by over a million dollars from 2009 to 2010.  Subsequently, a further cut of $138,000 was imposed on the school.  The school did not have the financial solvency to undo all the staff reductions in the PE department, counseling department, and other departments.  These reductions caused the class sizes throughout the school to swell over the limits.  The PE department will start the grievance process and will present it to the UBC; hence the UBC will become the presenter of said grievance.

*Action Item:  The UBC will present the grievance at the next SSC meeting.

– A member questioned the money given to the District by the State.  Why wasn’t the money used to mitigate these painful budget cuts, rescind the four furlough days, or rehire some laid-off teachers?  The response was that the District was saving the money for rainy days, when and if the State decided to further cut education fund next year.

– For some reasons, the District did not use the 10-day count figure to allocate the fall final budget to the school.  Because the District used a lower number, the school lost $138,000.  Members wondered what the site council could do to petition the discrepancy.  Even if the school couldn’t recoup the money, the school would learn how to prevent the same thing from happening in the future.  D. Page moved to send the District and Board of Ed a letter from the site council regarding the 10-day count discrepancy and how it impacted the budget allocations.  P. Delaney seconded the motion.  All voted in favor of the motion and the motion was passed unanimously.  M. Lau-Smith will draft the letter and send it to V. Pesek and K. Pringle for their input.  The letter will be presented at the next SSC meeting.

*Action Item:  The SSC will send a letter to the District and Board of Ed

– Other budgetary issues included:  (1) the site did not receive additional fund or resources for implementing the EL pathway; (2) the site had to pay for the textbooks used in the Special Ed department; (3) the science department uses more consumable supplies; therefore, their supply needs are greater.  V. Pesek had to use private donations to meet their needs.

4.  Members questioned the protocol of proposing items to the agenda and how to determine their appropriateness.  M. Lau-Smith commented that as long as the items are not personnel related, they should be permitted.  A member was already thinking about ways to getting more donations to Galileo for major repairs (i.e. telescope, etc.) or projects.

5.  A member requested an update on the Project Green.

D. Lee moved to adjourn the meeting.  D. Zeeman seconded the motion and the meeting was adjourned at 4:55 pm.

Minutes taken and prepared by Karin Lau
