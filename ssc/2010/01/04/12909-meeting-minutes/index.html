School Site Council Meeting-December 9, 2009

Present:  Vicki Pesek, Ben Chun, Stan DeBella, Doug Page, Bettie Grinnell, Mele Lau-Smith, Monica Eng, Linda Liu, Karin Lau, Zoe Duskin, Nancy Lambert, Katie Pringle

Absent (Excused): Kathleen Golata, Audrey Ricci, Leland Wong, Mark Huang

X  Minutes of the previous meetings approved

Decision Made/Issues Reviewed

I.  Call to Order

–B. Grinnell, SSC chair, called the meeting to order at 3:35 pm.  There were seven elected members present; a quorum was established.  The chair welcomed everyone at the meeting.

II.  Approval of 11/18/09 Meeting

-S. DeBella moved to approve the minutes as written.  D. Page seconded the motion, and all voted in favor of approving the minutes.

III.  Old Business

1.  Balanced Scorecard (BSC) Updates

– The person overseeing the development and implementation of the BSC is no longer working for the District.  Meanwhile, the District has asked all the schools to keep referring to the current plan until future notice.  The District is considering tweaking the framework of the future BSC.  Some of the graphics or icons may not be used or will be updated.

IV.  New Business

1.  School Programs Updates

– The District has decided to move up delivering the site’s prelim budget allocation on January 15, 2010 instead of waiting until mid-March.  The budget allocation is based on the projected enrollment for the fall of 2010.

– The State Department will audit our Student Nutrition Services within these two weeks.  If the District is found in compliance of their guidelines, some of the levied $2.1 million dollars may be restored to the District’s coffer.

– The full amount of the ADA revenue will be generated after the school enters all the attendance strips and the correlated excuse notes.

– Z. Duskin shared the updated professional development calendar with the site council.  This version had been reviewed and commented on by the leadership team.  The only change in this version is the postponement of the department presentations from April 2010 to April 2011.

2.  WASC Accreditation Process

– The WASC acronym stands for Western Association of Schools and Colleges, whose mission is to accredit schools and colleges for meeting certain standards, so their diplomas are meaningful and legitimate.  The WASC Visit will take place in spring of 2011.

– Z. Duskin elaborated on all the handouts inside the WASC folders, which were disseminated to all the council members:

1.  Model Timeline (Green):  Flow of Activities-Year Prior to Self-Study

(The Self-Study process will begin in January 2010.)

2.  Self-Study Overview (White):  Self-Study, Visit, Follow-Up

3.  Self-Study Report Format (White):  Chapter 1:  Student/Community Profile and Supporting Data and Findings; Chapter 2:  Student/Community-Overall Summary from Analysis of Profile Data; Chapter 3:  Progress Report; Chapter 4:  Self-Study Findings; Chapter 5:  School-wide Action Plan; Appendix

(Although findings will come from different sources and groups, Z. Duskin will be responsible for writing the final version of the report.  Therefore, a single writer’s style will be evident throughout the report.)

4.  WASC/CDE Focus on Learning, School-wide Criteria (Pink):  A.  Organization; B.  Standards-based Student Learning:  Curriculum; C.  Standards-based Student Learning:  Instruction; D.  Standards-based Student Learning:  Assessment and Accountability; E.  School Culture and Support for Student’s Personal and Academic Growth.

(All faculty and staff members as well as the site council members will be involved in two discussion groups-home groups are designated by their subject departments, and Focus on Learning groups-are based on one of the five “A” to “E” groups listed here.)

5.  The Self-Study Tasks Guide (White):  Task 1.  Refine student/community profile; identify 2 to 3 critical academic needs based on the analyzed and disaggregated data; clarify the expected school-wide learning results; Task 2.  Summarize progress since previous full self-study; Task 3.  Analyze the quality of the school program in relation to the WASC/CDE criteria with emphasis on the identified critical academic needs; synthesize the information, determine strengths and growth needs and identify potential action steps; Task 4.  Revise the comprehensive school-wide action plan; define school-wide and subgroup growth targets; establish an ongoing follow-up process to monitor implementation and accomplishment of the school-wide action plan.

(As the school moves through the self-study process, the administration will be referring to this guide for progress checks.)

-The school will aim for a clear 6-year accreditation, which is a rare occurrence but a possibility.  The next best thing will be a 6-year clear with a mid-term review.   The administration feels confident that the school will receive the highest rating because the school has developed the Balanced Scorecard based on the WASC criteria.

– D. Page volunteered to help with the gathering, disaggregating, and presenting of data for the process and report.  Z. Duskin encouraged anyone, including students, to participate in a WASC review to fully understand the process.  The opportunity will happen in the spring when the volunteers will be able to visit schools and write the final reports using specific rubrics, which can be brought back to the home schools for reference.

– Some teachers complained about the last WASC process.  The teachers had voiced their opinions through their discussion groups.  But when the final report was written, the report didn’t show much of their input.  Now the teachers wanted to know if their input would actually count and be included in the report.  It is paramount for all stakeholders to get involved in the accreditation process.  The visiting committee will ding the school if they sense that not everyone is involved.

V.  Open Forum

1.  Course Selections for 2010

-Counselors will begin visiting English classes to complete the course selections with the students in the early part of February.  The counseling office will pilot a program with the incoming 9th graders, who will be able to complete their course selections online.  It is the goal of the office to eventually have all the students complete this process via online only.

2.  The Site Council’s Role in Monitoring Approved Budget Items

-In order for the SSC to monitor their approved budget items, they need to create an accountability-check in system.  This system would require an approved item to be revisited at a future meeting for follow up.  Often times once an item is introduced and approved, it is never tabled for review at a later date.  For example, the SSC had approved a copious amount of State Compensatory Ed (SCE) funding to purchase a myriad of technological equipment (LCD projectors, document cameras, mimeo) and also to train teachers on how to use it effectively by having them create lesson plans with it.  For this instance, the SSC should have monitored the progress of the project more closely, but it didn’t.  This is not an isolated occurrence.  There were other approved budget items, i.e. Prop H Athletics, Prop H PE, Prop H Arts, LEP, etc., which the department heads had presented, and subsequently the SSC had approved, that never saw the light.  M. Lau-Smith volunteered to review all the past meeting minutes to seek out any of the past approved budget items that would require follow up revisits.  B. Chun motioned to calendar these potential items to future meetings, and to invite M. Bush, who managed the SCE funded project, to give the council a progress report at the January meeting.  L. Liu seconded the motion and all voted in favor of including these items in the January meeting.

3.  Let’s Compost!

– D. Page proposed that Galileo should implement a composting program. In addition to the obvious reasons-save the earth and teach our students to be responsible, the program could help the school lower its garbage bill. Though it might be an uphill battle with the students, we must try to instill that responsibility in them. There are other hurdles that may interfere with the success of the program.  The custodial staff is not required to empty the recycling bins because their contract says so.   There used to be a recycling program, in which Mr. McDowell’s students would go around the school to pick up recycling materials.  But that program ended because it got too physically strenuous and overwhelming for the students.  K. Lau cited the students’ resistance to recycling the paper containers and plastic bottles in the cafeteria.

-Regardless of the challenges, the first step would be for the school to hold an assembly to introduce the composting idea to the student body in the near future.   K. Pringle will investigate what the Department of Environment’s guidelines are regarding recycling and composting.  She will also see how the school can cooperate with the Golden Gate Disposable Co., who is contracted with the City to provide the recycling services.  To take a step further, she will also look into the solar panel program under the auspices of PG&E.

S. DeBella moved to adjourn the meeting.  D. Page seconded the motion and the meeting was adjourned at 4:55 pm.
