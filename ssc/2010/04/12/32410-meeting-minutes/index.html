April 12, 2010 Karin Lau Meeting Minutes

SAN FRANCISCO UNIFIED SCHOOL DISTRICT

Galileo Academy of Science and Technology

1150 Francisco Street, San Francisco, CA  94109

Room 210 at 3:30 P.M.

Location and Time of Meeting

School Site Council Meeting-March 24, 2010

X  Agenda Attached

X  Attendance/sign-in attached

Present: Ben Chun, Stan DeBella, Doug Page, Bettie Grinnell, Mele Lau-Smith, Audrey Ricci, Leland Wong, Linda Liu, Karin Lau, Zoe Duskin, Katie Pringle

Absent (Excused): Vicki Pesek, Kathleen Golata, Mark Huang, Jennifer Zhang

X  Minutes of the previous meetings approved

Decision Made/Issues Reviewed

I.  Call to Order

– B. Grinnell, SSC chair, called the meeting to order at 3:39pm.  There were eight elected members present; a quorum was established.  The chair welcomed everyone to the meeting.

II.  Approval of 2/20/2010 Meeting Minutes

– S. DeBella moved to approve the minutes as presented.  B. Chun seconded the motion, and all voted in favor of approving the minutes.

III.  New Business (Due to the guest presenters, this “new business” agenda item will come before the “old business” one.)

1.  Prop H Arts Preliminary Budget Planning

– J. Taylor, Department Head of the Visual and Performing Arts (VAPA) Department, presented his plans for the Prop H Arts fund of $20,636.00 for 2010/2011 by disseminating a handout to everyone.  The handout stated the department’s vision, which guided their spending objectives for the current year and helped shape their plans for next year.  The handout further stated that due to the shrinking resources, the department would change their focus from purchasing more equipment to maintaining what they have in their inventory.  Currently the department offers 29 sections of VAPA classes:  piano, guitar, band, orchestra, choir, drawing, painting, photography, computer art, media art, drama, AP music theory, and journalism/yearbook.  Due to the school’s budget constraints, the administration will not replace a retired VAPA teacher’s vacancy; therefore, the department will only be able to offer 19 sections next year.

– E. Winterling, Department Head of the Physical Education Department, who oversees the Prop H PE fund, commented that the Sports, Libraries, Arts, Music (SLAM) budget will continue to fund .40 FTE of a VAPA position.  J. Taylor validated the fact and stated that part of the 19 sections will come from this .40 FTE.

– The Prop H Arts budget is divided by the number of sections each teacher has, so the money is distributed equitably.  B. Chun questioned the impact of this fund on each teacher and what s/he buys with their allocation.  J. Taylor has all the information somewhere else and will be able to provide a detailed account at the next meeting.  Joseph did recall L. Douglas, who may retire next year, purchasing a class set of electrical pianos.  If he won’t be replaced, the equipment will be sitting there idly and silently.  J. Taylor is frustrated that the VAPA department has to take the brunt of the budget cuts.  B. Chun is disappointed that V. Pesek, the principal, is not present to hear the discouragement first hand.

2.  Prop H PE Preliminary Budget Planning

– E. Winterling, PE Department Head, oversees the Prop H PE fund of $36,793 for 2010/2011.  He presented the budget set up worksheet to the site council and the breakdowns are as follows:  Extended Hours/$6,793; Other Supplies/$16,800; Equipment/$5,000; Travel and Conference/$3,000; Rental and Leave Equipment/$1,200; Repairs and Maintenance/$4,000.  Unlike the VAPA department, where teachers get individual allocations based on the number of sections, the PE department shares everything with the exception of their individual laptops.  The $6,793 in extended hours will be used to pay him and Sergeant Hardee to enhance the JROTC program; some schools like Lowell High give teachers in that role a period.  It is more cost effective to pay teachers extended hours.  The money in supplies and equipment will be used to buy equipment for the fitness lab, cameras, video cameras, locks and an ice machine, which will be shared with the Associated Student Body and the cafeteria staff.  The amount in repairs will go towards fixing up the existing equipment due to its heavy usage by students and staff.  The travel and conference budget of $3,000 will send several teachers to attend the annual Physical Education Teacher Education (PETE) conference.

– Since the passage of the Prop H PE fund, Galileo’s PE department and the Athletics program have received substantial financial support for their improvements. It is crucial to strengthen the two programs until 2015, when the funding will end and when the school site will have to sustain all the improvements.  There is only one area remaining, which requires immediate attention and that is the dilapidated blinds in the Van Ness gym.  It would mitigate some financial pressure if the school could defer the cost over a 4-year period.  But most businesses don’t have installment plans.

– The SLAM office is looking to cut 30% to 50% of their budget.  The SLAM fund pays for 12 PE positions and Galileo has gotten one of the twelve teachers for the past few years.  But Galileo will not receive the one teacher next year and this will seriously impact the PE class sizes.

IV.  Old Business

1.  2010/2011 Preliminary Budget Proposal

– K. Pringle disseminated three budget handouts to the group-budget allocations, budget summary, and class size comparisons.  The budget allocations handout shows this year’s and next year’s budget allocations in the different funding sources.  The bottom line is that the school will receive $814,438 less next year.  The budget summary delineates a detailed account of all the funding sources for this year and next year as well as how the funds are used for personnel, non-personnel and extended services.  Although we anticipate getting $250,000 reinstated, the amount has not been included in this worksheet.

The class size comparisons handout compares the two years’ FTEs in different positions and how the fewer FTEs will impact class sizes in 2010/2011.  This handout shows only the FTEs funded by the site and that the class sizes are predicated on the projected enrollment of 2,150.  In the fall, the school will have a 10-day head count conducted by the district.  If the school shows an increase in enrollment, it will receive additional funding.

– 2.5 FTEs will be cut from the non-classroom staffing categories:  .25 FTE instructional aide and 2.25 FTEs secretaries. The district currently funds the college and career counselor, which is Ms. Boran, but they will not do so next year.  To continue these valuable services, the remaining five counselors will have to wear both hats as a grade counselor and a college/career counselor.  The breakdowns of the 2.25 FTE cuts in secretarial staff are:  .50 FTE/book clerk, .75 FTE/attendance clerk, and 1.00 FTE/secretary.  The school will have to do some reshuffling of duties to keep the school functioning.

– 6.85 FTEs will be eliminated from the classroom staffing categories.  5.85 FTEs came from teachers who will not return next year due to their retirements, which enable the school to consolidate only one filled position.  These cuts will affect the number of sections offered by departments.  With that variable and the actual enrollment being an unknown, these class sizes are only the best estimates:

1.  1.40 FTE cuts in Applied Science – class size to go from 42 to 45

2.  1 FTE cut in English – class size to go from 29 to 31

3.  1 FTE cut in Math – class size to go from 30 to 32

4.  1.60 FTE cuts in Social Studies – class size to go from 31 to 38

5.  1.20 FTE cuts in VAPA – class size to go from 31 to 42

6.  1 FTE cut in World Language – class size to go from 29 to 34

7.  .50 FTE cut in Other Electives – class size to go from 33 to 30

8.  .25 FTE addition in JROTC

9.  .60 FTE addition in Science – class size to go from 36 to 37

– Both K. Pringle and Z. Duskin would like the site council to recommend how the school should allocate the $250,000 when the amount would be reinstated.  The amount will almost be enough to hire three teachers, but it would be shortsighted to spend it all on personnel when the school has nearly nil operational expenses.  One member suggested hiring 2.5 teachers and the rest be placed in non-personnel.  Another member supported the suggestion and further proposed that the 2.5 FTEs should be a math teacher, a social studies teacher, and a half something else.  One member wanted the council members to bear their priorities in mind, which were to preserve teaching positions, so to keep class sizes manageable, and in turn to support student learning.

– B. Chun randomly perused students’ schedules and realized that many students doubled up on their VAPA classes; therefore, jamming these classes.  He also commented that students often take Health Ed, a graduation requirement, in the senior year; and therefore, not allowing younger classmen to take that course.  Students should take Health Ed earlier in their high school career like in the 9th grade, so that they could make wiser and healthier choices later on.  If 9th graders were to take this course, the school would need to create 15 sections.  Currently, there are only 5 sections in place; 10 more sections are needed to meet the demands.  At this juncture, there are 712 new enrollees, of which 672 are incoming 9th graders.

– After much discussion, the council unanimously agreed that the administration should try to keep class sizes small and manageable, be equitable in creating sufficient number of course sections to meet the student needs, and maintain a minimal operational expenses.

V.  Open Forum

– B. Chun informed the council that the development of a software program on FileMaker Pro, which will be used to keep inventory of the school equipment, is nearly done.  The software program was designed with the types of reports the school needs to generate such as what equipment is available, what equipment is broken/stolen/retired, etc.

D. Page moved to adjourn the meeting.  B. Chun seconded the motion and the meeting was adjourned at 5:11 pm.
