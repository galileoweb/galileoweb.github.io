March 3, 2010 Karin Lau Meeting Minutes

SAN FRANCISCO UNIFIED SCHOOL DISTRICT

Galileo Academy of Science and Technology

1150 Francisco Street, San Francisco, CA  94109

Room 210 at 3:30 P.M.

Location and Time of Meeting

School Site Council Meeting-February 10, 2010

X  Agenda Attached

X  Attendance/sign-in attached

Present:  Vicki Pesek, Ben Chun, Stan DeBella, Doug Page, Bettie Grinnell, Mele Lau-Smith, Audrey Ricci, Leland Wong, Mark Huang, Linda Liu, Jennifer Zhang, Karin Lau, Zoe Duskin, Katie Pringle

Absent (Excused): Kathleen Golata,

X  Minutes of the previous meetings approved

Decision Made/Issues Reviewed

I.  Call to Order

– B. Grinnell, SSC chair, called the meeting to order at 3:37pm.  There were eleven elected members present; a quorum was established.  Jennifer Zhang introduced herself; she will replace Monica Eng for the spring semester.  The chair welcomed everyone to the meeting.

II.  Approval of 11/18/09 Meeting

– S. DeBella moved to approve the minutes as presented.  L. Liu seconded the motion, and all voted in favor of approving the minutes.

III.  Old Business

1.  School Programs Updates

– P. Theel from Career Technical Education (CTE) department announced that due to her budget reduction, her program could only fund 1.40 FTE (from 2.80 FTE) of our pathway and academy teachers for the school year 2010-2011.  If the school couldn’t make up the difference, some programs might have to be eliminated.  But how does the school decide which programs to cut?

– Galileo will increase the projected enrollment for 2010-2011 from 2,089 to 2,150 based on the following reasons:  Galileo has become one of the sought after schools; therefore, there is a long waiting list of students wanting to get in; Newcomer School will be disbanded; therefore, many of the students may opt to attend Galileo; a slightly higher enrollment will generate more funding for the school; therefore, the increase will off set the imminent budget cuts.

– The school needs to continue to do outreach to attract a diverse population; families that get assigned to Galileo may be asked to do community hours to support the school.

– J. Taylor, department head of the Visual & Performing Arts department, is concerned that there is a lack of electives, which puts students in a dilemma of choosing between pathway/academy classes verse arts classes.  The District suggests offering College and Career in the 9th grade instead of 10th.

2.  WASC Accreditation Process Updates

– There was a full day of professional development on the WASC process held on 1/29/2010.  All the focus groups met for the first time to begin looking at their roles and responsibilities.  At the end, faculty members gave positive feedbacks on how the day went.

– Z. Duskin disseminated the handout-“School-wide Criteria, WASC/CED” and “Criteria Concepts, WASC/CDE”, which defines the involvement of the site council in the WASC process more clearly.  B. Chun questioned the differences between the Local Educational Agency and the School Site Council.  Z. Duskin clarified that the governing board has (a) policies and bylaws that are aligned with the schools’ purpose and support the achievement of the expected school-wide learning results and academic standards based on data-driven instructional decisions for the school; (b) delegates implementation of these policies to the professional staff; and (c) monitors results regularly and approves the single school-wide action plan and its relationship to the Local Educational Agency (LEA) plan.

–  The administration selected the window of dates for the WASC Visit, which will be between the CA Exit Exam and spring break of 2011.

*Point Person:  Z. Duskin

Proposed Action:  Two chapters of the WASC report will have been written by April 2010.

3.  Recycling and Composting Regulations/Updates

– At one time the Board passed a resolution to increase the rate of recycling across the district.  Galileo has a 28% of recycling rate; an outsider goes through the trash and extracts all the redeemable bottles.  Mission High has a much higher rate.  Galileo lacks a willing teacher to sponsor the recycling club.  Many science teachers have turned down the sponsorship; S. DeBella will check with his science club to see if they are interested.  Before a club is officiated, there is the possibility that we can ask one or two students from each classroom to bring the blue bin to the big dumpster.

– Eiei has approached the Earth Club and Youth Council for ideas.  K. Pringle has tried to get the Department of the Environment to come to Galileo to present an assembly to promote composting.  There is a stipend of $200 per year to any custodian who supports the composting effort.  Custodians are not contracted to empty the blue recycling bins into the big dumpster; therefore, the contents go into the garbage bin.  Currently, A. Wattles, a teacher, has a composting program for worms in her classroom.  She can accept raw produce to feed them.

–  The grant from PG&E will power one classroom, not enough juice to support the school’s needs.  The work required for the grant writing and the matching fund will discourage pursuing it.

*Point Persons:  K. Pringle and A. Ricci

Proposed Action:  Present the PG&E report at the March meeting

4.  SCE Funded Classroom Technology Updates

– At the January site council meeting, Z. Duskin presented a chart outlining the roles and responsibilities of G. Carter, B. Chun, T. Ly, and Z. Duskin in the technology integration program.  Since then, another piece has been added, which is K. Pringle will oversee the inventory of the school equipment.

– On April 6, 2010, the school will be audited on its attendance and financial procedures.  With the finances, the auditor will want to know how the school handles them and what procedures are in place.  Therefore, from this juncture, K. Pringle will take on a first-hand approach with the school budget.  She will update each transaction on the Quicken files to provide a running balance of each object code.  J. Tavisora will continue to do the ordering and receiving.  K. Lau will support K. Pringle and continue to prepare the budget report.  Transparency and efficiency will be the goals for all the reshuffling of responsibilities.

*Point Person:  K. Pringle

Proposed Action:  Present the inventory process at the March meeting

IV.  New Business

1.  Budget Updates

– The Weighted Student Formula (WSF) budget is frozen.  The school can still submit requests, but the expenditures must be justified on the form-“Request for Exemption from Expenditure Freeze”.  Though the school can still submit requests, V. Pesek will scrutinize each item to ascertain that the expenditure is urgent and essential.  The unspent balances will be used to help address the District’s shortfall.

*Point Persons:  K. Lau and V. Pesek

Proposed Action:  Present the preliminary budget for 2010/2011 at the March meeting

2.  Calendaring Approved Budget Items for Review

– In the future whenever the site council approves an item, it should have a follow-up plan and a point person.  In fact, this proposed practice would be in place immediately.  M. Lau-Smith will review the past two meetings’ minutes and add any approved items to the chart, which will make the chart current.  She will also review the chart to see which items require a follow-up plan or deem closed and complete.

*Point Person:  M. Lau-Smith

Proposed Action:  Present the up-dated chart at the March meeting

3.  Guidelines for Requesting Extended Hours

– V. Pesek and K. Lau established the guidelines for staff to request for extended hours:  1.  The services are performed beyond the school day; 2.  There are no other funding options; 3.  The services add more responsibilities to the person’s workload.  D. Page suggested the fourth point, which is the services are directly related to the curriculum and that they support the Balanced Scorecard and WASC’s objectives.  V. Pesek gives the final approval.  The site council requested for these guidelines to be established so that the practice can be equitable to all.

*Point Person:  K. Lau

Proposed Action:  The guidelines will be emailed to the site council

V.  Open Forum

1.  The District sent out a memo recently about installing filters at all the schools’ drinking fountains instead of purchasing Arrow Head bottled water.  The council members questioned which would save the district more?  They also were concerned if the filters would actually screen out all the debris in our drinking water. 

*Point Person:  K. Pringle

Proposed Action:  Report findings at the March meeting

2.  A parent from another school is looking to rent a stage.  Our auditorium is not used daily and is available for rental.  The requests are processed at the District’s real estate office and they keep all the rental fees, not the school sites.  What the organization can do is pay the District the rental fee and also make a donation to the school site.

S. DeBella moved to adjourn the meeting.  D. Page seconded the motion and the meeting was adjourned at 5:01 pm.

Minutes taken and prepared by Karin Lau
