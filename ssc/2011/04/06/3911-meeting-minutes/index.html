Present:  Doug Page, Jonathan Ring, Woodraw Smith, David Zeeman, Patrick Delaney, Mele Lau-Smith, Claire Siegel, Nicole Torres, Diane Lee, Frank Wu, Michelle Zhang, Nancy Lambert, Karin Lau, Katie Pringle, Gayline Tom, Marcia Lomnett

Decision Made/Issues Reviewed

I.  Call to Order

– M. Lau-Smith, chair, called the meeting to order at 3:41 pm.  There were eleven elected members present; a quorum was established.

II.  Approval of 2/9/2011 Meeting Minutes

– D. Page moved to approve the minutes as presented.  P. Delaney seconded and the motion was carried unanimously.

III.  Old Business

1.  WASC Reminder

– The WASC visiting committee will commence the accreditation process by attending the welcome reception on Sunday, March 13, 2011 and will wrap-up the process by holding a report-back session on Wednesday, March 16, 2011.  The Academy of Hospitality and Tourism students will host and help out at both functions.  Parents were asked to attend the welcome reception on Sunday and the parent group interview on Monday.

VI.  New Business

1.  Spring Prelim Budget Allocations

– K. Pringle passed out the following four handouts and discussions among the attendees followed:

i.  Cost of Positions-3 year cost comparisons (2009/2010/2011) of the administrators, certificated, and classified staff.  For example, the total cost of a teacher (salaries and benefits) for 3 years:  2009 at $80,961, 2010 at $84,861, 2011 at $86,550.

ii.  Summary of Funding Sources-Charts of the different funding sources Galileo received in 2009, 2010, 2011 Scenario A and 2011 Scenario B.  For example, the grand total for 2009 was $9,149,783, 2010 was $9,011,381, and 2011A was $9,483,763 and B was $8,631,327.

– The prelim budget allocations did not show the school received any AP or newcomer specialty funding.  Though Galileo got some newcomer money this year, J. Fong, Director of English Learner Support Services, did not indicate if Galileo would receive it next year.  A member suggested that we keep track of this line item in our fall final allocation in case we have to ask the District to reimburse us.

– It is difficult to tell if the District shortchanges Galileo, because their budgeting process lacks transparency.  And when they do underfund the school, they will likely not reimburse us.  For example, the District did not allocate the AP money to the school this year, which is roughly around $90,000, and the District will most likely not reimburse us.  The only recourse is for UBC to file a grievance.  The letter that the school will send to the Board of Ed should include this issue.  When that happens, the school will send the Board members a strong message about this unfair practice.  Z. Duskin suggested that when we submit the prelim budget, we should highlight this issue to bring it to the District’s attention.

– Other high schools receive substantial amounts of categorical funding, but Galileo does not.  With the elimination of the Targeted Instructional Improvement Grant (TIIG), we are getting the biggest hit of all the high schools.

iii.  District & School Site 2011 Review-A 3-year comparison of the non-classroom staffing pattern, teachers by departments staffing pattern, sections/class sizes by departments.

– In scenario A of 2011/2012, the number of FTEs goes up because the school will allow students to double up in some of the math and science classes (the students didn’t double up this year due to budget cuts). The number of sections by departments and class sizes go up slightly because the school anticipates a higher projected enrollment.

– In scenario B of 2011/2012, the number of FTEs decreases.  The admin does not want to consolidate any teachers; therefore, they will not fill any openings vacated by staff who will leave their jobs voluntarily due to retirement, maternity leaves, sabbatical leaves, etc. Z. Duskin, who is an assistant principal, is in one of these openings.  She will resign at the end of the school year to move to Washington D.C.  Galileo will be setting a precedent of having two assistant principals with an enrollment over 1,500.  When Scenario B becomes a reality, how will two APs manage three people’s responsibilities?

– Another opening goes to an ELD teacher, who will retire in May.  A member questioned how the ELD department could function without having this teacher replaced.  Members all agreed that when the SSC chair signed off on the budget that she needed to keep all of these issues in mind.

iv.  Budget Summary-A 3-year comparison of each funding source*, which was broken down into:  amount allocated, amount budgeted, balance, total FTEs (full time equivalent), salaries, benefits, extended services, non-personnel.

* funding sources-unrestricted general fund, Targeted Instructional, special ed, EIA/State Compensatory Ed (SCE), EIA/Limited English Proficient (LEP), Prop H PE, Prop H Arts

– In the scenario A budget, there will be $270,613 for non-personnel expenditure and in scenario B $127,690, but that is after pillaging into the LEP fund.  Since the current year’s non-personnel expenditure is $252,218, the site council would like to know if next year’s amount of $270,613 can be reduced further by $86,000 (amount needed for a full-time position).  Z. Duskin assures the council that there are sufficient supplies to meet the school needs; therefore, the site council should plan on creating the open position.  J. Ring moved to increase the FTE allocations in scenario A by reducing the non-personnel amount by $86,000 and approve the scenario B budget under duress.  N. Torres seconded the motions and they were approved unanimously.

2.  UBC Petition Re:  M. Chiu

– The School Site Council would like to support the UBC’s petition to bring former principal and current assistant superintendent, Margaret Chiu, back to Galileo to fill the principal position.  J. Schultz feels that it is a decision M. Chiu has to make.  If she is interested, she can follow the procedure and submit a letter of intend; therefore, she will become one of the six candidates, whom Galileo will interview on April 16.   P. Delaney moved to support the UBC’s petition to bring M. Chiu back.  D. Page seconded the motion and it was carried unanimously.  P. Delaney offered to approach M. Chiu about the petition.

3.  Administrative Selection Process

– J. Schultz, assistant superintendent, who oversees 15 of the 18 high schools, is the guess speaker to talk about the administrative selection process.  Introductions went around the room.

– Every year around this time, the District will advertise all their administrative openings.  They also recruit from the administrative credential programs at Stanford and Berkeley and by word of mouth.  The District then does their initial screening; they look for specific requirements such as the applicants’ years of administrative and teaching experiences, letters of references, transcripts, proper credentials, etc.  These are the external applicants.  For the internal applicants, all they have to do is to submit an updated resume and a letter of intend.  From all of these avenues, the District will have their pool of applicants. Subsequently the District will form a panel comprised of assistant superintendents to hold group and one-on-one interviews and rate each applicant using their rubrics.  Based on these ratings, Richard Carranza, the deputy superintendent, will look at the qualifications of the applicants and the needs of the schools and assign applicants to the schools that will yield the best matches.  These assigned applicants will ultimately be the pool of candidates that the site selection committees interviews on Saturday, April 16, 2011.

– Prior to April 16, 2011, each site forms their own administrative selection committee with the school community members self-nominating themselves.   For a high school like Galileo, the administrative selection committee is required to have 1 school administrator, 2 certificated staff members, 1 classified staff member (a second one optional), 2 parent/community members (a third one optional), and 2 students, who are School Site Council reps.   At the same time, the School Site Council formulates the site- specific interview questions and forwards them to the District.  The Human Resources office will have their district-specific questions, which will be merged with the site’s and become the final interview questions.

– Starting at 8:30 am on Saturday, April 16, 2011, the administrative selection committee will interview up to six candidates.  The process will be facilitated by one of the assistant superintendents.  All the candidates are asked the same pre-selected questions.  At the end of the day, the administrative selection committee can take any one of the following actions:  rank the top three candidates, rank the best one, rank the best two, or request to interview another pool of applicants.  When all the interviews are completed, all the assistant superintendents will match these top-ranking applicants to the schools and submit their recommendations to the superintendent. The superintendent will make the final decisions.  He can go with the recommendations or override them.  If the first round of interviews does not work out, the school can hold another round, which will likely be in mid-May.

– When two or more schools rank the same candidate as one of their top choices, the assistant superintends will make their recommendations to the superintendent based on the “best match” quotient.

– The Central Office chooses the pool of candidates for each school.  The school’s site council does not get involved in this process.

– The UBC recommends that Galileo hire the principal first.  When the principal is in placed, s/he can have her/his input in the selection of the assistant principal.  J. Schultz feels that this strategy may backfire because when Galileo is ready to hire their assistant principal, all the strong candidates may not be available.  If the school misses the window of opportunity to interview potential applicants, the central office will appoint someone to fill the vacancy.

D. Page moved to hold the next SSC meeting a week earlier on Wednesday, April 6, 2011 instead of April 13, 2011.  W. Smith seconded and the motion was carried unanimously.

D. Page moved to adjourn the meeting.  D. Zeeman seconded and the meeting was adjourned at 6:35 pm.
