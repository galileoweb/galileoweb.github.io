February 2, 2011 Karin Lau Meeting Minutes

SAN FRANCISCO UNIFIED SCHOOL DISTRICT

Galileo Academy of Science and Technology

1150 Francisco Street, San Francisco, CA  94109

Room 210 at 3:30 P.M.

Location and Time of Meeting

School Site Council Meeting-January 12, 2011

X  Agenda Attached

X  Attendance/sign-in attached

Present: Doug Page, Jonathan Ring, Woodraw Smith, David Zeeman, Patrick Delaney, Mele Lau-Smith, Claire Siegel, Nicole Torres, Diane Lee, Frank Wu, Henry Pan, Zoe Duskin, Nancy Lambert, Karin Lau, Marcia Lomneth

Absent (Excused): Vicki Pesek

X  Minutes of the previous meeting approved

Decision Made/Issues Reviewed

I.  Call to Order

– M. Lau-Smith called the meeting to order at 3:34 pm.  There were eleven elected members present; a quorum was established.  Introductions were conducted around the room.

II.  Approval of 12/8/2010 Meeting Minutes

– C. Siegel moved to approve the minutes as presented.  J. Ring seconded and the motion was carried unanimously.

III.  Old Business

1.  School Site Council Bylaws

– M. Lau-Smith sent around the final version of the SSC Bylaws for the members to view and comment.  All the proposed changes had been added and/or modified.

– K. Lau pointed out that “Article III-Membership, Section 1-Composition and Size, Part D-The SSC will have at least these minimum number of members” were not consistent with the actual practice.  The Bylaws will be amended to reflect the actual composition of membership.  D. Page moved to amend the Bylaws as proposed and accept the Bylaws as revised.  J. Ring seconded and the motion was carried unanimously.

– D. Lee went over the ASB Bylaws, which showed that when the students are elected to the Business Vice President and Student Body Advisory Council rep positions, they automatically serve on the SSC.  That will leave the third position for the general student body to vie for.  The site council supported the continuation of the same practice and proposed no changes to their Bylaws.

– The site council strategized on the recruitment of candidates for this third position.  They felt that the teachers should encourage all students to run so that the position will be equally represented.  K. Lau will hold the elections for the parent reps as well as the student rep.

2.  Van Ness Bus Transit Project Update

– H. Pan didn’t have any new information for the site council since the last SSC meeting (on January 12).  He will have more details to report after he attends the next County Transportation Authority meeting on February 22nd.  Meanwhile, he will try to get the director of the CTA to attend the February SSC meeting.

VI.  New Business

1.  UBC’s Grievance

– The UBC did not make any progress with the PE class size grievance because the problem may have been mitigated.  Since swimming is no longer a graduation requirement and the swimming class had a very low enrollment of 17 only, the admin closed the class and created a new PE class.  With the added section, the admin was able to give those crowded PE classes some relief.

2.  Project Green Update

– K. Pringle passed out a copy of the project green layout titled:  Preferred Schematic Design Perimeter Landscaping.  The project will add low maintenance native plants and trees along the Polk Street, Francisco Street, and Van Ness Avenue sides of the main campus.  The project will also include new benches and planters on the north side.

– L. Franzen, the Environmental Science teacher, will be the lead person of the project.  The grant must be all used up by the summer or it will be gone.

3.  Telescope Repair

– In order to make Galileo more like its namesake-science and technology, the school needs to step up its marketing effort and market the school as a science and technology campus.  At the same time, the school should work on building relationships with the science and astronomy departments at all the universities and colleges throughout the bay area.   Strong relationships may lead to grant opportunities to repair the telescope and develop programs and internships for the school.

– The magnitude of the telescope repair is extensive.  Besides getting the telescope to function properly, the work must include the installation of an elevator so that the area will be ADA accessible.   The cost to install an elevator runs between $250,000 to $300,000.  There will also be auxiliary expenses such as engineering reports, architectural reports, etc.  The school may want to scale down the project and to focus on the repair of the telescope first.  In that case, the cost will be around $25,000.

– There are many wonderful ideas floating around.  It is important to have a group of people to research the project further.  So what is the mechanism to set up a committee?  The proposal can be brought to the faculty to solicit participation and to the admin to look at the marketing purposes.

*Action Item:  The admin will report back any development of the proposal in March or April.

4.  AP Preps and Budget Implications

– There was a meeting concerning the future of the Advanced Placement (AP) prep periods at the union headquarter, in advance of contract negotiations.

– AP classes generate a lot of revenue and FTEs for the school.  For every 20 exams taken, the school would get $16,972.20 (.20 of an FTE of $84,861) over and above the regular budget allocation and .20 FTE (one teaching period).  At Galileo, there were 636 AP exams taken last year.  That meant the school should have received $543,110 (636 rounded up to 640, divided by 20 = 32 times $16,972.20) in revenue and 6.40 FTEs.  But, of course, the school didn’t show any gain.  K. Pringle tried to inquire about this AP money from the Budget Office, but she was deferred to Ms. Waymack in the Policy and Ops district office.  K. Pringle would try to meet with Ms. Waymack to get clarifications and invite her to attend a SSC meeting when she could explain the AP money in person.

– The union and high school reps have always tried to get the District to be more transparent with their budgeting process; it is an ongoing challenge.

– Other SFUSD high schools have teachers as AP coordinators to oversee their programs.  Galileo doesn’t have one; N. Lambert, our assistant principal, wears that hat as well.  Last year, she only received a tiny rebate of $500 for all those 600 plus AP exams given.

– J. Ring and K. Pringle would add this particular issue to the letter that the Site Council is sending to the District and BOE re: Fall allocation.  The letter should be finalized at the next meeting.

*Action Item:  K. Pringle will meet with Ms. Waymack and invite her to a SSC meeting.  Finalize the letter.

5.  WASC Action Plan

– Z. Duskin gave the members a copy of the WASC action plan, which is a working draft of the action plan for the WASC Accreditation Process.  The draft plan consisted of five action items, which merged from different sources-student data, parent input, focus groups findings, etc.  The five action items are as follows:

i.  Developing Newcomer Pathway

ii.  Improving Outcomes for African American and Latino students

iii.  Supporting the 9th Grade Transition

iv.  Strengthening Instruction

v.  Sustaining and Strengthening Professional Learning Community

– Z. Duskin asked the SSC members to study the Action Plan and be prepare to discuss the document at length at the next SSC meeting.  Until then, she will share the document with all the stakeholders (i.e. PTSA, Student Executive Council, etc.) to get their input.

– The WASC Visit will commence on Sunday, March 13 at 2:00 pm at a welcome reception hosted by the Academy of Hospitality and Tourism (AoHT).  The Leadership Team and the SSC members are required to be present.

Action Item:  Review the Action Plan and be prepared for an in depth discussion.

6.  Budget Transfers (Prop H PE and Prop H VAPA)

– K. Lau mentioned that the department heads are currently doing a mid-year review of their budget allocations and moving funds around to meet their evolving needs.

7.  Addendum Items:

i.  Galileo recently installed a vending machine, which sold District pre-approved items such as beef jerky, granola bars, juice drinks.  All the proceeds will be split amongst the ASB and four classes.

ii.  Mr. Wing is spearheading a homecoming class reunion event on Saturday, September 24, 2011.  The day will include a football game at 1:00 pm, dinner at 5:30 pm, auction and entertainments thereafter.  The reunion will be a joint event from all the different classes coming together to celebrate instead of from a single class.

iii.  If teachers need to use a color printer, they can go to T. Ly in Room 260, the math department office in Room 352, or H. Machtay in Room 101.

iv.  The student reps would like to know if they could get on the February agenda, because they would like to ask the SSC for cash donation to help offset their graduation expenses.  M. Lau-Smith had to take a look at the protocol of such request before it could be granted.

D. Zeeman moved to adjourn the meeting.  W. Smith seconded and the meeting was adjourned at 5:02 pm.

Minutes taken and prepared by Karin Lau
