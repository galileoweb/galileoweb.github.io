January 6, 2011 Karin Lau Meeting Minutes

SAN FRANCISCO UNIFIED SCHOOL DISTRICT

Galileo Academy of Science and Technology

1150 Francisco Street, San Francisco, CA  94109

Room 210 at 3:30 P.M.

Location and Time of Meeting

School Site Council Meeting-December 8, 2010

X  Agenda Attached

X  Attendance/sign-in attached

Present: Doug Page, Jonathan Ring, Woodraw Smith, David Zeeman, Patrick Delaney, Mele Lau-Smith, Claire Siegel, Diane Lee, Frank Wu, Henry Pan, Zoe Duskin, Karin Lau, Gayline Tom

Absent (Excused): Vicki Pesek, Nicole Torres, Michelle Zhang

X  Minutes of the previous meeting approved

Decision Made/Issues Reviewed

I.  Call to Order

– M. Lau-Smith called the meeting to order at 3:35 pm.  There were ten elected members present; a quorum was established.  Introductions were conducted around the room.  M. Lau-Smith welcomed G. Tom, who is the PTSA president and has two children attending Galileo.

II.  Approval of 11/10/2010 Meeting Minutes

– C. Siegel moved to approve the minutes as presented.  W. Smith seconded the motion, and all voted in favor of approving the minutes.

III.  Old Business

1.  WASC Update

– Z. Duskin explained the WASC Process.  To read the file in its entirety, go to the official website at www.acswasc.org/pdf_general/WASC_ProcessOverview.pdf.

Below is the snippet of the WASC Process from the website:

The Ongoing Process

• Self-Study • Visit • Follow-Up

Summary: Self-Study Process

␣ What? What is the ideal based upon…?

• Expected schoolwide learning results (student goals)

• WASC criteria

• Academic standards or curricular objectives ␣ So what?

What currently exists and how effective is it? ␣ Now what?

What and how will we modify? What should be in the schoolwide action plan?

Findings

1.  Areas to celebrate — yet maintain

2.  Areas that need development and growth

Lead to…direct alignment with growth areas and strengths and action plan

The Visit

Three to eight educators

Usually 3-1⁄2 days

Visiting Committee report based on:

␣ Self-Study

␣ Findings during visit

␣ WASC criteria (or WASC/___ joint criteria, e.g., WASC/ACSI, WASC/CDE)

Term Factors

•Six years: progress report, one-day or two- day mid-term review for some schools

•Three years: progress reports, three year two-day visit to extend term one year, three years or deny

•One or two years: progress reports, one-day visit to extend term up to three years or deny

•Denial: reasons detailed in Visiting Committee report

– The self-study report contains 6 chapters, which derived from the 5 focus groups’ findings over the past year.  The school admin will present this draft of self-study report at the PTSA meetings to get parents’ input.   At the same time the admin will survey the students for their input and will use their work as supporting evidence.   The findings in these five focus groups-Organization and Governance, Curriculum, Instruction, Assessment, School Culture-are sorted in two areas:  General Strengths and Areas for Growth; and any common issues that repeat throughout these two areas will serve as the bases for the school’s Action Plan.  Each area will have no more than 5 items for the staff to work on and the total action plan will not be more than 6 pages.

– Z. Duskin passed out the draft findings of the 5 focus groups and asked the council members to review them from now until the next meeting, when the members can present and share their comments.

*Action Item:  Review the draft findings and report back comments

2.  School Site Council Bylaws

– M. Lau-Smith had reviewed the Bylaws beforehand and wrote down some suggested changes.  She went over her suggestions with the council and they were as follows:

i.  Article III – Membership and Election.  Council members debated whether to maintain the conventional two-year terms or stagger terms.  D. Page moved to change to stagger terms and W. Smith seconded the motion.  All voted in favor of staggering the terms.  For student representatives, F. Wu moved to make the student term one year and P. Delaney seconded the motion.  All voted in favor of keeping the student term to one year.

– Currently, the students elected to certain offices for the Associated Student Body also serve as the SSC student reps.  D. Zeeman moved to make the student representation on the SSC be free from the ASB Bylaws.  P. Delaney seconded the motion and all voted in favor of disassociating the ASB Bylaws from the SSC Bylaws.

– P. Delaney moved to make the most vote getters to become the alternates and when there aren’t alternates, a general election will be held.  C. Siegel seconded the motion and all voted in favor of the motion.

ii.  Article VII – Meetings.  P. Delaney moved to change the mode of communication to emailing and any documents need reviewing need to be attached to the email for previewing.  F. Wu seconded the motion and all voted in favor of the motion.

– For items to be added on the agenda, P. Delaney moved to have an item placed on the agenda at a previous meeting, or one week before a meeting, or at an agenda review at a said meeting; and to eliminate the current Open Forum item.  H. Pan seconded the motion and all voted in favor of the motion.

– J. Ring moved that decisions be made by a majority vote of seven members.  P. Delaney seconded the motion and all voted in favor of the motion.

– The council members discussed what the best way would be to capture public comments.  Members suggested that the chair recognize the public speaker during the agenda review to add their agenda item.  Then they are allowed 2 minutes to speak.

3.  Revised Budget Presentation

– K. Pringle had prepared the revised budget 2010-2011 spreadsheet as requested by including last year’s data and comparing it with this year’s; also by including percentages and full time equivalence (FTE) where possible.  She couldn’t be at the meeting, so Z. Duskin substituted and explained the three sections of the spreadsheet-sections one (non-classroom) and two (classroom) are staffing allocations, and section three are the numbers of sections, numbers of students, and class sizes by departments.  The notable reductions came from the VAPA and PE departments.  Z. Duskin deferred all questions until the next meeting when K. Pringle herself could further explain the data.

VI.  New Business

1.  UBC’s Grievance

– The UBC will file a grievance on class size violation.  The item will be postponed to the January meeting.

2.  Board of Ed Letter

– The council members proposed to send the grievance letter and the Board of Ed letter simultaneously to the powers to be.  When M. Lau-Smith finished drafting the letter, she would email it to P. Delaney so he could include the grievance letter with it.  The school could send these two letters once the new Board of Ed members took offices.

3.  Project Green Update

– Per the school district, the school couldn’t build a roof garden because it lacked accessibility for all students.  The school would proceed to scale down the initial proposal.  Construction will start next semester because the grant money must be spent in this school year.  Once done, the school has to be responsible for its maintenance.

V.  Open Forum

– H. Pan explained the Van Ness Bus Transit project to the council.  The project involves the Transit Authority installing a bus lane all along Van Ness Avenue.  A rep from the transit agency will make a presentation to the site council in the February meeting.  The rep can also attend any or all of the homeroom meetings so the students will be aware of its implication.

– D. Zeeman suggested that the school needed to step up its effort to market itself to the community, especially the affluent Marina District.  His focus is to raise money to repair the telescope.  D. Page commented that it would be a costly endeavor, and if the structure could not allow access to everyone, it might not be possible.

*Action Item:  D. Zeeman proposes to explore fund raising ideas to repair the telescope

P. Delaney moved to adjourn the meeting.  C. Siegel seconded the motion and the meeting was adjourned at 5:06 pm.
