October 30, 2008 Karin Lau Meeting Minutes

Galileo Academy of Science and Technology
1150 Francisco Street
San Francisco, CA  94109

Room 210 at 3:45 P.M.
Location and Time of Meeting

School Site Council Meeting-October 1, 2008

X  Attendance/sign-in attached

Decision Made/Issues Reviewed

I.  Welcome/Introductions
–Ethan Winterling welcomed everyone and called the meeting to order at 3:23pm.  A quorum was established.

II.  Old Business
1.  Review and Approve the Fall Final Allocation
— Karin Lau passed out the Fall Final School Site Budget handout and went over it object code by object code, budget resource by budget resource.
–Vickie Pesek recapped the decisions, which had impacted the budget to its current state; and, notably the recommendation made by Jonathan Ring and Curtis Chinn, which was a reduction of enrollment for the 2008/09 school year.  With the decrease of approximately 200 students, the school received roughly $560,000 less in allocation.  In the same vain, the school didn’t have to fill three vacant positions, but were required to create two .6 FTEs positions in other over-crowding areas.  Subsequently, the school was able to meet everyone’s non-personnel spending needs.
–The school received an additional PE teacher who was funded by the District.
–The school also had the Doug Piper donation to support its technology needs.  Unfortunately, the Kim Foundation donation didn’t pan out.
–Bettie Grinnell moved to approve the Fall Final Budget Allocation as presented to the site council.  Mele Lau seconded the motion.  All was in favor of passing the budget as it is written.

III.  New Business
1.  SSC Election
–Karin Lau had been recruiting guardians/parents/community members to serve on the School Site Council.  So far, Leland Wong and Mele Lau had agreed to continue their services, but the school still needed one more member to meet its composition requirement.  The school’s UBC rep would begin its classroom and non-classroom elections soon.

IV.  Open Forum
–The Tech Committee needed someone to oversee the school’s technology development.
–When the bungalows were removed, the blacktop would remain and serve as a staff parking lot.  The football field would get a facelift with either a natural or synthetic turf installed.  There were pros and cons with these two material types and the choice preferences were hotly debated in the District.

Bettie Grinnell moved to adjourn the meeting; Jonathan Ring seconded the motion, and the meeting was adjourned at 4:49 pm.
