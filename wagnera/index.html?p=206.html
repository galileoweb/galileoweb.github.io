Analytical Response to “Howl”

Writers

In connection with all other components studied in and out of class you will explore the significance of Allen Ginsberg’s most famous piece of writing. First, read “Howl” and respond to these questions in preparation for a Socratic Seminar.

Now, consider this: What do Ginsberg’s words say about the Focus Author you chose to study? What do his words say about the Beats and their generation? Your write-up should be no more than one page.

Editors

After perusing your writers’ words, compile them into a concise response to Allen Ginsberg’s “Howl.” Do not omit too much content or style, but work to merge their ideas. A sentence here, a sentence there, eventually making up a united piece of no more than two pages.
