Venezuela

March 16, 2005 Copernicus Uncategorized

  • Biblioteca Virtual Miguel de Cervantes: Venezuela
    Excellent resource site in Spanish for the country of Venezuela. The
    flag and a general map of the country are included. Of particular
    interest is the statistical data available on land size, climate,
    population, life expectancy, infant mortality rate, and economic data.
    Independence day, ethnic and religious groups, languages, and monetary
    unit are also provided. More detailed information on constitutions and
    government may prove useful to students with advanced skills in reading
    Spanish.

  • Venezuela, A History
    A very good history of Venezuela.

  • A Map of Modern Venezuela
    A full-color map of modern Venezuela.

  • The Washington Post: Venezuela
    The Washington Post covers the latest news, issues and events in
    Venezuela. Content also includes internet references to find out more
    about the country.

  • Encyclopedia Encarta: Venezuela
    An encyclopedic discussion of the land, climate, natural resources,
    plants and animals, population, principal cities, education, culture,
    economy, trade of Venezuela. Also mentions the Venezuelan government,
    Venezuelan history which includes World War II and postwar politics,
    the Perez Jimenez Regime, Democratic governments, Nationalization
    measures, privatization measures, and Chavez’s Rule. There are also a
    few pictures.

  • Venezuela’s Famous People
    A short list of famous people who were born in Venezuela along with an accompanying biography on Simon Bolivar.

  • CultureGrams: Bolivarian Republic of Venezuela
    Fantastic introduction to the culture of Venezuela. This is a paysite,
    and a thorough cultural description is available only through a
    subscription or a payment to the site. Nevertheless, interesting,
    up-to-date information is provided free of cost. A good map and a
    picture of the flag are provided, together with a few interesting
    cultural observations, and statistical data. The national anthem is
    also available for listening.

  • 8 Capítulos para Entender la Historia de Venezuela
    Main page for the history of Venezuela from Venezuelatuya.com. Gives a
    history of Venezuela from the Pre-Colombian period to the present. The
    links are brief but informative. There are many illustrations, making
    the site visually appealing.

Do you have additional resources which you would like to see on
this page? Will you share any experience you have had with this country
with other readers? Are you from this country? Please share with us. We
look forward to hearing from you.
