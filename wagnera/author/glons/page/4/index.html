Argentina

March 16, 2005 Copernicus Uncategorized

  • Slide Show for the country of Argentina
  • Biblioteca Virtual Miguel de Cervantes: Argentina
    Excellent
    resource site in Spanish for the country of Argentina. The flag and a
    general map of the country are included. Of particular interest is the
    statistical data available on land size, climate, population, life
    expectancy, infant mortality rate, and economic data. Independence day,
    ethnic and religious groups, languages, and monetary unit are also
    provided. More detailed information on constitutions and government may
    prove useful to students with advanced skills in reading Spanish.
  • CIA World Factbook: Argentina
    This
    site contains wonderful information on Argentina. A large, detailed map
    is provided along with great facts and figures of the country. This
    site is a great site to check out on the subject.
  • Encyclopedia Encarta: Argentina
    Encyclopedia
    Encarta provides a detailed overview of the country of Argentina.
    Content explores the land and resources, population, education,
    culture, economy, government and history of the country.
  • Tiscali Travel: Argentina
    This
    travel site explores the country of Argentina. Content is available on
    this country’s major cities, general environment, cutlure, history, and
    more.
  • Argentina
    Review
    Argentina’s destination guide to get information on travel, events,
    money and costs, attractions and activities. This site has many
    colorful photos to give you a first hand look at this beautiful country.
  • Argentina News
    Argentina
    News is a web resource that pulls its content from a variety of very
    well renowned sources. Coverage includes a look at the top headlines of
    the day, as well as sports and travel news.
  • Commanding Heights: Argentina History Timeline
    A
    comprehensive timeline of Argentine history involving the military
    takeovers in the latter part of the 20th century. Also includes recent
    efforts to bring Argentina back to civilian rule after the Falkland
    Island War.
  • A Brief History of Argentina
    A
    brief history of the political arena in Argentina. Includes information
    on Peron and the military regime that took his place after the
    ill-fated presidency of his wife, Isabel Peron.

Do you have additional resources which you would like to see on
this page? Will you share any experience you have had with this country
with other readers? Are you from this country? Please share with us. We
look forward to hearing from you.

Slang Dictionaries

February 1, 2005 Copernicus Uncategorized

Very few janky dawgs are ever off da hook.  I’m not trying to be a
hater, I’m just saying that it’s rare for them to kill it with their
words, and their steelo is usually pretty bunk.  So yo, if you
want to get down with some sick terms and avoid being a tool, you have
to keep it real with yourself about what’s going down around you,
aiight?  If you play it straight you’ll have a mad crunk lexicon,
and before long you’ll be flossin’ to all your friends. 

What keeps language alive is that it is always changing.  
Evidence of this lies in the dictionary–most recently, the term “bling
bling” was added, a term I’m sure my parents don’t use, let alone
know.  In an effort to appreciate the creative language found in Catcher in the Rye,
by J.D. Salinger, you will be studying the slang of your times. 
Listen to the conversations you hear in the halls, in the classroom,
and in your head–what are the words and phrases that distinguish your
conversations from those of…well, old people.  Determine how
these words and phrases came to be, and become an expert in their
usage. 

For each word or phrase you hear, you must do the following:
1.  Write out the word, including it’s phonetic spelling
2.  State the etymology
3.  Indicate all appropriate parts of speech, with necessary spelling changes
4.  Provide a sample sentence, showing the variations of usage

Finally, compile all of your words, alphabetically, into a Slang
Dictionary and submit your findings.  Your due date will be
announced in class.

Programa de Estudios para Español I/II

August 30, 2004 Copernicus Uncategorized

Los objetivos:

  • Develop active listening skills
  • Develop basic vocabulary
  • Develop interactive communication skills
  • Develop a solid foundation in the basics of Spanish grammar
  • Develop an appreciation for the Spanish-speaking world, its cultures and civilizations

Las expectativas:

  • Respect yourself and others.  The classroom is a place where
    everyone should feel safe and able to participate without fear or abuse
    of any kind.
  • Arrive to class on time and be in your seat when the bell
    rings.  If you must be tardy, enter the room quietly and without
    disturbing others.
  • Bring your books, notebook and pens everyday.  Be prepared.
  • Participate in classroom activities and assignments.
  • Have fun!

I take the rules of this class and this school very seriously and I
would like you to do the same.  I want you to feel free to speak
your mind; in fact, I encourage it, but not if it is at the expense of
one of your classmates or me.

Los avios:

  • 8 ½ by 11 inch spiral notebook with 200+ pages, and 5 pockets (5 Star brand is recommended)
  • Pens (blue or black ink) and pencils with erasers
  • Highlighters 
  • Glue stick
  • Computer (or similar apparatus) with a constant flow of ink, paper, and reliable discs! (Galileo computer labs are recommended)

El libro de texto:

¡Bravo!

Las tareas:

Las hojas de autoevaluación (50 puntos por semana)
Las evaluaciones de escuchar y comprender (puntos varian por semana)
Las pruebas y los exámenes (puntos varian por lección y por unidad)
Los cuadernos (100 puntos por plazo )
Las actividades de clase y la ética laboral (puntos varian)

I expect you to complete every assignment for this class!  In
fact, it will be difficult to pass this class unless you turn in every
assignment.  Any and all excuses for late work must be cleared
with me.  I do not accept extra credit.

Las notas:

90 – 100% de los puntos = A
80 – 89% de los puntos = B
70 – 79% de los puntos = C
60 – 69% de los puntos = D
59% y bajo = F

Las ausencias y las tardanzas:

In
order for you to learn, you must be present and on time for
class.  It is impossible to make up the direct instruction and interactive activities that are
covered in the classroom.  If you know in advance that you are
going to absent, see me immediately for outside work.  In the
event of an unexpected illness or tardiness on the day of a major
assignment, you must call me immediately at 415/749.3430 x3360, and
provide a written note from your doctor upon your return.  Late
work for major assignments will not be accepted otherwise.  In the
case of any absence, you are still responsible for all work covered in
class.  Copy down the names and numbers of at least two other
class members in case you need to inquire about missed work.

If by some chance you are absent or late for class, I expect you to do the following:

La ausencia

1. The day you return to school, you must have a written excuse from your parent/guardian.*
2. Your
note must be processed by the attendance office and the readmit slip
you are provided in exchange must be signed by me.
3. Inquire about all missed work from classmates—it is your responsibility.

*See attendance office for a more complete description of school-wide procedures.

La ausencia sin excusa


Missed assignments can only be made up
if absences are excused.  If you fail to excuse your absence, you
will be required to complete an Unexcused Absence Pass and turn it in
to me.  Inevitably, unexcused absences will result in a lowered
grade.

La tardanza

1. Enter quietly.
2. Complete and turn in a Late Pass.
3. Quietly get out your materials and begin to participate.
4. Ask a classmate for any missed information after class.

Please, once you realize that you are going to be late, do not
compound the problem by disrupting the class.  Inevitably,
excessive tardiness will result in a lowered grade.

This course requires the involvement and enthusiasm necessary for studying a new language; your
organization, regular and punctual attendance and diligence is
required.   For your benefit, I am available for outside help
during routinely scheduled office hours.  I hope you find this
course enlightening and rewarding.

To verify that you read and understand this information, go to Syllabus Agreements.
