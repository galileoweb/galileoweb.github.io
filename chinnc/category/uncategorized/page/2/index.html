Review Sheet for Test on Chapter 7 

 

Nucleus                                                                        Ribosome                                Hypotonic

Prokaryotic Cell            Facilitated Diffusion                  Endoplasmic Reticulum             Hypertonic

Eukaryotic Cell             Equilibrium                               Golgi Apparatus                    Active Transport Diffusion                      Osmosis                                   Vacuole                                  Endocytosis

Passive Transport                                                         Mitochondrion                         Exocytosis

Organelle                                                                      Chloroplast                             Pinocytosis     

Cell Membrane                                                             Cytoplasm                                Isotonic           

                                               

 

  1. What are the parts of the cell theory
  2. what is the difference between prokaryotic and eukaryotic cells
  3. Where are proteins made?  What type of organelle manufactures the proteins.
  4. What organelle makes new membrane?
  5. That organelle sorts, packages and ships proteins?
  6. What organelles transform energy from sugar into energy that our cells can use?
  7. What organelles absorb sunlight and store them as sugar?
  8. What are the levels of organization in humans?
  9. What are the differences between a plant and animal cells?
  10.  What are 3 things that all cells have?
  11. What is diffusion?
  12. What is the difference between osmosis and diffusion? 
  13. What is the difference between active and passive transport?
  14. If you have a hypertonic solution and you put a hypotonic cell which way will water move?
  15. How do large molecules enter a cell?

 

Lab Questions:

  1. If I tested a membrane and put glucose and starch inside a tube.  How would I test to see if glucose can move through the membrane?  How could I test if starch could go through the membrane?  What would the results look like if I glucose can go through?  What would the results look like if starch could go through?
  2. If I had a cell that was hypertonic to a solution, and I place it in that solution for 10 minutes what would happen?  What would happen if I had a cell that was hypotonic to a solution?  How would I measure any changes?

Chapter 11

Who is Mr. Chinn?

September 8, 2005 Copernicus Uncategorized

Mr. Curtis Chinn was born in San Francisco.  He is a 4th generation San Franciscan which explains why he doesn’t speak Chinese very well!  He went to school at Commodore Sloat Elementary, Aptos Middle, and Lowell High School.  He went to college at U.C. Davis, and majored in Biological Sciences and Dramatic Arts.  He also has studied Education at San Francisco State University and Theology at Golden Gate Baptist Theological Seminary.

He started teaching at Galileo as a Student Teacher in 1995.  He’s been teaching Biology here ever since.  Mr. Chinn has had fun being a Galileo Lion for more than a decade.  He really enjoys seeing how the school has improved and seeing students go on to great things. 

He got married in 2005 to his wife Vivian.  They live with their cat in the Richmond District of San Francisco.  No kids yet, but as soon as they have kids, they will let you know.
