Information about Biotechnology Pathway

Biotechnology is one of the youngest fields of biology.  It is the use of living things or their products in industries such as pharmaceuticals (medicines), medicine, criminology, national defense, environmental science, and industrial design.

The use of biotechnology in a very simple form has actually been around for thousands of years, dating back to the use of yeast or bacteria to make things like bread, beer and yogurt.  Modern biotechnology is only a few decades old, and involves manipulating the DNA of organisms and making proteins or other products.

It’s a good topic to study for many reasons:  1) The biotech industry is on the cutting edge of science, finding cures and treatments for illnesses or improving human life.  Exciting discoveries are being made everyday.  2)  The biotech industry is expanding rapidly with companies hiring many people right out of college or sometimes even with only a 2 year degree from a Junior College.  3)  Biotechnology companies pay well and offer a unique lifestyle compared to many other corporate jobs.   4)  Many of the first biotech discoveries in biotechnology were made right here in the Bay Area, and many of the top university labs and private companies are located within 5o miles of San Francisco.

Personally, I really enjoy teaching Biotechnology.  I think it is a great class because it takes what you learn in biology and chemistry and applies it.  We do more labs than we do book work.  A lot of the labs we do, don’t have a “right” answer, but instead we allow students to come up with ways of making the procedures better.  For me as a teacher it is fun to see the students learn enough to be able to conduct their own experiements.

So far we have been learning the basic techniques of biotechnology.   That includes learning how to make plates for bacteria to grow on, how to distribute the bacteria on the plates to get different patterns for analysis.  But this is just the beginning, from here we will be changing the DNA of the bacteria to get it to make different proteins.  Some of these proteins will make the bacteria different colors or even glow in the dark.  Aside from being interesting, this technique is useful to get bacteria to make proteins that humans need like Insulin for people with diabetics.

The class is not “hard” in the sense that it has lots of tests or homework, but it is a challenge to do the labs.  It invovles working well with others, planning ahead, figuring out how to do things on your own, coming afterschool or during lunch to do labs, and starting over if a lab doesn’t turn out the way you expect it to… sometimes we’ll do a lab 2,3,4 even 5 times until we are satisfied with the results.  This can be frustrating, but I think you learn a lot from labs that don’t work at first.  It’s a process.

For students interested in taking Biotechnology, we require that you take Biology and get at least a B.  You should plan on taking Chemistry either before or at the same time as Biotech.  You can take Biotech in either you sophomore, junior or senior year.  But if you are interested in the pathway, you should start either your sophomore or junior year.   This year we have 2 sections of Biotech I, which is the first year, and we have about 30 students per class.  The second year of Biotech has 24 students, mostly seniors.

This year we plan on taking some of our students to Genentech, one of the biggest local biotech companies.  During the summer, some of our students get internships to further their studies.  Besides going out, we try to bring in guest speakers to share about their careers in biotech.
